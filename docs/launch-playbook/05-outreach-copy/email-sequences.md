# Email Sequences (Ready-to-Use)

> Direct outreach emails to researchers. Personalize every email.

## Sequence 1: Cold Outreach to Systematic Review Authors

### Finding Targets
1. Search Google Scholar for: `systematic review [topic] 2025 2026`
2. Find the corresponding author's email (usually in the paper or their university page)
3. Read their abstract — you need to reference their specific work

### Email 1: Initial Outreach

```
Subject: Your systematic review on [specific topic] — question about your process

Hi [First name],

I came across your systematic review on [specific topic] published
in [journal/venue]. The [specific thing — e.g., "evidence mapping
across 47 studies"] was particularly impressive.

I'm building a research tool called Lumen that's designed for exactly
the kind of work you do — structured evidence synthesis with full
traceability. The core idea: every claim maps to source + page + quote,
contradictions between sources are surfaced explicitly, and evidence
is tagged by confidence level.

I'm looking for 30-50 experienced researchers to be "Founding
Researchers" — use the tool for real work and help shape how it
handles complex synthesis tasks.

Would you be interested in taking a look? It's completely free
(lifetime access for Founding Researchers), and I'd genuinely
value your perspective given your experience with systematic reviews.

No pressure either way — I just think you'd find it relevant
to your workflow.

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Email 2: Follow-Up (5 days later, if no response)

```
Subject: Re: Your systematic review on [specific topic]

Hi [First name],

Quick follow-up on my message last week. I know how busy research
gets, so I'll be brief:

Lumen's core differentiator is contradiction surfacing — it actively
identifies where sources disagree, rather than averaging everything
into a smooth summary. For systematic reviews, this means catching
the nuances that matter for evidence grading.

If you have 5 minutes, here's a quick look at how it works: [link
to a short demo or screenshot]

Happy to answer any questions. Either way, good luck with your
research.

Best,
[Your name]
```

## Sequence 2: Outreach to PhD Students (Warmer Channels)

### Finding Targets
1. University lab websites — look for PhD students with listed research interests
2. Twitter/X — PhD students who tweet about their lit review process
3. Reddit — users who post in r/PhD about research workflows

### Email Template (adapt per person)

```
Subject: Thought of you — new tool for lit review synthesis

Hi [First name],

I noticed your [tweet about / Reddit post about / research on]
[specific reference]. The challenge you described — [paraphrase
their specific frustration] — is exactly what I've been building
a tool to solve.

Lumen is a research workspace that turns scattered papers into
structured synthesis:
- Claims mapped with evidence confidence levels
- Contradictions between sources surfaced explicitly
- Gaps in the evidence identified
- Everything traceable to source + page + quote

I'm running a Founding Researcher program — 30-50 researchers
who use Lumen for real research and help shape the product.
Lifetime free access, direct input into what we build.

Since you're [in the middle of your lit review / working on X],
it might be useful right now. Want to try it?

Application: [link]

Either way, I hope [their research topic] goes well.

[Your name]
```

## Email Best Practices for Researcher Outreach

### Do
- Reference their specific research (proves you're not mass-emailing)
- Keep it under 200 words
- Use a personal email address, not a marketing tool
- Send Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM their timezone
- Follow up exactly once (after 5 days)
- Make the ask low-friction ("take a look" not "commit to a program")

### Don't
- BCC a list of researchers
- Use marketing language ("revolutionary," "game-changing")
- Claim Lumen will "replace" their workflow
- Follow up more than once
- Send from a no-reply address
- Use HTML-heavy email templates (researchers use plain email)

### Expected Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Open rate | 40-60% (subject line + personal sender) |
| Response rate | 15-25% (well-personalized) |
| Application rate | 50-70% of respondents |
| Volume | 5-10 emails per day (hand-crafted) |
| Timeline | 50-100 total emails over 3-4 weeks |
