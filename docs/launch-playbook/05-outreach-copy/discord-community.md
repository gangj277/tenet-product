# Discord Community Setup

> The Founding Researcher community hub.

---

## Why Discord (Not Slack)

- Free for unlimited members and message history
- Better for community (channels, threads, roles, voice)
- Researchers are increasingly on Discord (especially in CS, ML, and younger academics)
- Easier to manage than email threads

---

## Server Structure

### Channels

```
📋 INFORMATION
├── #welcome          — Auto-message on join + rules
├── #announcements    — Product updates, new features, milestones
└── #faq              — Common questions and answers

🔬 RESEARCH
├── #introductions    — Founding Researchers introduce themselves + their work
├── #show-your-work   — Share synthesis outputs, interesting findings
├── #research-tips    — General research methodology discussion
└── #tool-talk        — Discussion about research tools (not just Lumen)

💬 FEEDBACK
├── #bugs             — Bug reports (structured template)
├── #feature-requests — What they wish Lumen could do
├── #wins             — Things that worked well
└── #friction         — Things that felt confusing or slow

🏗️ BUILDING
├── #changelog        — What shipped this week
├── #roadmap          — What's coming + discussion
└── #office-hours     — Schedule and notes from live sessions

☕ GENERAL
├── #off-topic        — Anything not research or Lumen
└── #introvert-corner — Low-pressure async chat for quieter members
```

```

You're one of [X] researchers shaping the future of evidence
synthesis. Here's how to get started:

1️⃣ **Introduce yourself** in #introductions
   Share: your name, research area, and what you're working on

2️⃣ **Try Lumen** if you haven't already
   [Link to your account]

3️⃣ **Share feedback** as you use it
   - #wins for what works
   - #friction for what doesn't
   - #bugs for things that broke
   - #feature-requests for what's missing

4️⃣ **Join office hours** (biweekly)
   Next one: [date and time]

Ground rules:
- Be kind. We're all here because we care about research.
- Be honest. Critical feedback is the most valuable kind.
- Be specific. "This didn't work" is less helpful than
  "When I tried X, Y happened instead of Z."

Questions? Tag @Founder anytime.
```

## Office Hours Format (Biweekly, 30 min)

### Structure
- **First 5 min**: Quick demo of what shipped since last time
- **Next 15 min**: Open Q&A — researchers ask anything
- **Last 10 min**: "What should we build next?" discussion

### Rules
- Keep it informal. This isn't a corporate all-hands.
- Record and share notes for people who can't attend
- Encourage researchers to show their screens and demo their workflows
- Time-zone rotate: alternate between US-friendly and EU/Asia-friendly times

---

## Engagement Tactics

### Prompt Conversations (Don't Let the Server Go Quiet)

**Monday question prompts** (rotate weekly):
- "What's the most surprising contradiction you've found in your research?"
- "If you could automate one part of your lit review process, what would it be?"
- "What's a research tool you can't live without? (Besides Lumen 😄)"
- "Describe your evidence synthesis workflow in 3 steps."

### Celebrate Wins
When a researcher shares a success:
```
This is amazing, [name]. The fact that Lumen helped you catch
[specific finding] is exactly why we're building this.

Mind if we share this (anonymized) as a testimonial later?
```

### Handle Frustration Gracefully
When someone reports a problem:
```
Thank you for flagging this, [name]. That sounds genuinely
frustrating.

Can you share:
1. What you were trying to do
2. What happened instead
3. Any error messages or screenshots

I'll look into it today.
```
