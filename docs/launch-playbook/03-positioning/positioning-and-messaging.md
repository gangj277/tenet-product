# Positioning & Messaging

## April Dunford Positioning Framework (Applied)

### Step 1: Competitive Alternatives
What would our best customers use if Lumen didn't exist?

1. **Manual synthesis** — Read papers, take notes in Google Docs/Notion, build mental models by hand
2. **Elicit + Zotero + Google Docs** — Use Elicit to find papers, Zotero to organize, Docs to write
3. **NotebookLM** — Upload PDFs, ask questions, get grounded answers (but no structure)
4. **Consensus** — Get quick yes/no answers from research (but no depth)
5. **ChatGPT/Claude with uploaded PDFs** — General-purpose AI chat over documents (hallucination risk, no provenance)

### Step 2: Unique Attributes
What capabilities do alternatives lack?

| Attribute | Why It's Unique |
|-----------|----------------|
| Contradiction surfacing | Actively identifies and displays where sources disagree — no competitor does this |
| Evidence provenance chain | Every claim → source → page → quote → confidence level. Full traceability |
| Multi-persona consolidation | Analytical, skeptical, methodology-focused perspectives examine evidence from different angles |
| Structured artifact system | Discrete outputs (claims, gaps, next-steps) — not a blob of text or a chat log |
| Perspective inference | Understands your research frame, intent, and evidence criteria before searching |
| Workspace persistence | Files you can edit, organize into folders, and build on over time |
| Proposed-update workflow | Agent suggests edits via inline diffs that you accept or reject — you stay in control |

### Step 3: Value (and Proof)

| Attribute | Benefit | Proof Point |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Contradiction surfacing | Never miss a disagreement that could invalidate your argument | Beta tester: "Found 3 contradictions in my lit review I'd been ignoring for months" |
| Evidence provenance | Defend every claim in your paper with traceable citations | Every claim links to source + page + quote |
| Multi-persona consolidation | See your evidence from angles you wouldn't think to check | 4 analytical personas each produce independent findings |
| Structured artifacts | Go from "I've read everything" to "here's what the evidence says" in hours, not weeks | 5 discrete outputs ready to reference while writing |
| Perspective inference | Get results relevant to YOUR research question, not generic summaries | Pipeline adapts discovery, analysis, and synthesis to your specific frame |
| Workspace persistence | Build cumulative understanding — don't lose context between sessions | Files, notes, papers, experiments persist and organize |

### Step 4: Target Market Characteristics
Who cares most about this value?

**Primary segment (highest pain intensity):**
> PhD students and postdoctoral researchers who are writing systematic reviews or comprehensive literature reviews, who currently spend 40+ hours manually reading, extracting, and synthesizing evidence from dozens of papers, and who need every claim in their output to be traceable to a specific source.

**Secondary segment:**
> Research consultants and think tank analysts who are paid to deliver evidence-based recommendations under tight deadlines, and who need to quickly survey a research landscape they may not be deeply familiar with.

### Step 5: Market Category

**Positioning style: Big Fish, Small Pond**

We are NOT positioning as:
- "An AI research assistant" (too crowded — Elicit, SciSpace, Consensus all claim this)
- "A better search engine for papers" (not what we do)
- "AI for literature review" (too generic)

We ARE positioning as:
> **The AI research workspace for evidence-grounded synthesis**

This is a category *upgrade* from "AI research assistant." Existing tools assist with tasks (finding papers, extracting data). Lumen is a *workspace* where the synthesis happens — with structure, provenance, and judgment.

## 3 Key Messages (Benefit-Driven)

### Message 1: "Never miss a contradiction again"
Lumen actively finds where your sources disagree — the critical nuances that make or break your argument. No more discovering in peer review that you overlooked a contradicting paper.

### Message 2: "Every claim traceable to source, page, and quote"
No hallucinated citations. No vague attributions. Every piece of evidence in your synthesis links back to exactly where it came from, with a confidence level attached.

### Message 3: "From scattered papers to structured understanding in hours"
Lumen produces five discrete artifacts — overview, synthesis, claims map, gaps analysis, and next steps — so you go from "I've read everything" to "here's what the evidence says" without weeks of manual work.

## Messaging Do's and Don'ts

### Do
- Lead with the pain: "scattered papers," "buried contradictions," "can't articulate what the evidence says"
- Emphasize *synthesis* vs. *extraction* — this is the key differentiator
- Use "evidence-grounded" and "traceable" — these build trust with academic audiences
- Show humility: "AI-assisted, researcher-controlled"
- Acknowledge the tool landscape: "You already use great tools for finding papers. Lumen picks up where they leave off."

### Don't
- Claim to "replace" researchers or their judgment
- Use "revolutionary" or "game-changing" — academic audiences are allergic to hype
- Compare directly to ChatGPT — it cheapens the positioning
- Promise to "do your literature review for you" — researchers want to be in control
- Use emojis or exclamation marks in academic-facing copy
