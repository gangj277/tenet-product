# Founding Researcher Program

> The core of Lumen's CBT strategy. Not "beta testers" — **co-builders**.

---

## Philosophy

The Founding Researcher Program is built on one insight: **the best early users aren't people who test your product — they're people who shape it.** We don't want bug reporters. We want researchers who care deeply about how evidence synthesis should work and want to help build the tool they wish existed.

The program name matters. "Beta tester" implies they work for you. "Founding Researcher" implies they're part of the origin story. This framing:
- Attracts higher-quality participants (people with opinions, not just curiosity)
- Creates psychological ownership ("I helped build this")
- Gives them social capital to talk about later ("I was a Founding Researcher at Lumen")
- Justifies asking for deeper engagement (feedback calls, detailed reports)

---

## Program Design

### Cohort Size
- **Target: 30-50 Founding Researchers** in the first cohort
- Quality threshold: Better to have 25 deeply engaged than 50 with half inactive
- Cohort-based (not rolling) to create shared experience and community

### Duration
- **12 weeks** (one academic quarter)
- Week 1-2: Onboarding + first research project
- Week 3-8: Active use + feedback cycles
- Week 9-10: Deep feedback interviews
- Week 11-12: Retrospective + graduation

### What Founding Researchers Get

| Perk | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Lifetime free access** | Full Lumen access forever, including all future features |
| **Direct founder access** | Weekly office hours, direct messaging, input on roadmap |
| **"Founding Researcher" badge** | Permanent badge on their Lumen profile |
| **Name on the wall** | Listed on Lumen's website as a Founding Researcher (with their consent) |
| **Priority feature requests** | Their requests go to the top of the backlog |
| **Early access to everything** | Every new feature, they see it first |
| **Research credit** | If Lumen ever publishes about its methodology, Founding Researchers are acknowledged |
| **Community** | Private Discord channel with other Founding Researchers + the team |

### What Founding Researchers Commit To

| Commitment | Frequency | Time |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| Use Lumen for at least one real research project | Throughout the 12 weeks | Varies |
| Complete a weekly feedback form (5 questions) | Weekly | 5 min |
| Participate in one 30-min feedback call | Once during weeks 9-10 | 30 min |
| Report bugs and friction points as they encounter them | Ongoing | As needed |
| Share one honest testimonial at the end (if they genuinely liked it) | Once | 10 min |

**Total estimated time commitment beyond their normal research**: ~2 hours over 12 weeks.
This is deliberately low — we want signal from their natural workflow, not artificial engagement.

## Selection Process

### Application Flow
1. Researcher finds recruitment post (Reddit, Twitter, email, etc.)
2. Clicks link to a short application form (see below)
3. Application reviewed within 48 hours
4. Selected researchers receive a personalized welcome email
5. Onboarding call or async onboarding guide (see [Onboarding Playbook](./onboarding-playbook.md))

### Application Form (Keep It Short — 5 Questions)

```
1. What's your current research focus? (1-2 sentences)

2. What stage of research are you in?
   [ ] Literature review / systematic review
   [ ] Writing a paper or thesis
   [ ] Starting a new research direction
   [ ] Other: ___

3. What tools do you currently use for research synthesis?
   (e.g., Elicit, Zotero, NotebookLM, Google Docs, pen and paper)

4. What's the most frustrating part of your current research workflow?
   (2-3 sentences — be specific)

5. Why do you want to be a Founding Researcher?
   (1-2 sentences)
```

**Selection criteria**: See [Screening Criteria](./screening-criteria.md)

## Tiers Within the Program

Not all Founding Researchers need the same depth of engagement. Offer two tiers:

### Tier 1: Founding Researcher (Standard)
- **Count**: 30-40 researchers
- **Commitment**: Weekly feedback form + use Lumen for real research
- **Perks**: Lifetime free access, badge, name on wall

### Tier 2: Founding Researcher (Advisory)
- **Count**: 5-10 researchers (invite-only from Tier 1 after 4 weeks)
- **Commitment**: Monthly 1:1 calls, detailed workflow documentation, roadmap input
- **Additional perks**: Listed as "Advisory" on website, invited to product design sessions, first to review major feature plans
- **Who**: Tier 1 researchers who show exceptional engagement and have strong opinions about research workflows

The Advisory tier is *earned, not applied for.* After 4 weeks, identify the most engaged Founding Researchers and personally invite them to upgrade.

## Communication Cadence

### Weekly
- **Monday**: Send weekly feedback form via email (5 questions, rotating topics)
- **Wednesday**: Post a "What we shipped this week" update in Discord
- **Friday**: Share one insight from the feedback in Discord ("You told us X, here's what we're doing about it")

### Biweekly
- **Office hours**: 30-min live session where Founding Researchers can ask questions, share frustrations, or demo their workflows

### Monthly
- **Product update email**: What shipped, what's coming, how their feedback shaped it
- **Cohort metrics**: Share aggregate stats ("this month, Founding Researchers synthesized X papers across Y projects")

## Graduation & What Comes After

At the end of 12 weeks:

1. **Feedback interview** (30 min) — deep-dive into their experience
2. **Testimonial request** — only if genuine ("Would you be comfortable sharing your experience?")
3. **Founding Researcher status becomes permanent** — badge, access, name on wall
4. **Invitation to stay in the community** — Discord remains active
5. **Option to join Advisory tier** (if not already)
6. **Referral ask** — "Know 2-3 colleagues who'd benefit from Lumen?"

### Graduation Metrics (Per Researcher)
- Did they complete at least one full research project in Lumen?
- Did they submit 6+ weekly feedback forms?
- Would they recommend Lumen to a colleague? (NPS question)
- Did they discover something in their research they would have missed without Lumen?

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

| Don't Do This | Do This Instead |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Accept everyone who applies | Be selective — quality of feedback depends on quality of researchers |
| Treat them like test subjects | Treat them like co-builders with agency and opinions |
| Only ask about bugs | Ask about workflows, frustrations, "what would you do if Lumen could..." |
| Go silent between feedback cycles | Constant communication — they should feel the momentum |
| Ignore their feedback | Close the loop: "You said X, we built Y" |
| Incentivize with money | Incentivize with ownership, access, and recognition |
| Make the commitment too heavy | 2 hours over 12 weeks. Their main job is *doing their research* with Lumen |
