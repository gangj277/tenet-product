# Onboarding Playbook

> Turn accepted Founding Researchers into active, engaged users within their first 48 hours.

---

## The Activation Goal

A Founding Researcher is "activated" when they have:
1. Created their Lumen account
2. Completed one full research project (question → synthesis)
3. Read at least one generated artifact (synthesis, claims, or gaps)
4. Interacted with the workspace agent at least once

**Target**: 90%+ of accepted researchers reach activation within 7 days.

---

## Onboarding Flow

### Day 0: Acceptance

**Trigger**: Application approved

**Actions:**
1. Send welcome email (template in [Founding Researcher Program](./founding-researcher-program.md))
2. Create their account with pre-approved access
3. Add them to the Discord #founding-researchers channel
4. Log them in the cohort tracker (spreadsheet or Notion)

### Day 1: First Project

**Trigger**: They log in for the first time

**In-app experience:**
1. **Welcome screen**: "Welcome, [Name]. Let's start your first research project."
2. **Guided prompt**: "What research question are you currently working on?"
   - Placeholder: "e.g., What is the evidence for mindfulness interventions in reducing academic burnout?"
3. **Option to upload PDFs**: "Have papers already? Upload them here."
4. **Pipeline runs** → They see progress (discovering sources, analyzing, synthesizing)
5. **Perspective review**: Lumen shows what it understood about their research intent → they confirm or adjust
6. **Synthesis delivered**: 5 artifacts appear in their workspace

**First-run tips (non-intrusive, dismissible):**
- "Tip: Click any claim to see the source, page, and quote it's based on."
- "Tip: Try asking the agent '/devils-advocate' to challenge your synthesis."
- "Tip: You can add your own notes and organize files into folders."

### Day 3-7: Deepening Engagement

- **Day 3**: Share a "Getting the most out of Lumen" guide in Discord
- **Day 5**: Post first weekly feedback form
- **Day 7**: Check activation metrics. Personally reach out to anyone who hasn't completed a project.

## Getting Started Guide (In-App or Email)

### "5 Things to Try in Your First Week"

**1. Run a real research question**
Don't use a test question. Use something you're actually working on. Lumen is built for real research, and the feedback you give will be most valuable if it's grounded in your actual workflow.

**2. Read the Claims artifact**
This is where Lumen maps out the key claims from your sources, ranked by evidence strength. Check if the confidence levels match your own reading of the papers.

**3. Look at the Gaps artifact**
Lumen identifies what the evidence doesn't cover — questions your sources don't answer. These are often the most interesting outputs.

**4. Challenge your synthesis**
Open the agent chat and try:
- "/devils-advocate" — Lumen will argue against your synthesis
- "/methodology-critic" — Lumen will assess the quality of your evidence
- "What contradictions did you find?" — Ask directly

**5. Edit and make it yours**
The synthesis, claims, and gaps are all editable. Add your own notes, adjust claims, or write new files. Lumen is a workspace, not a read-only report.

## Common First-Session Problems (and How to Address Them)

| Problem | Likely Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| "It didn't find relevant papers" | Research question too broad or too niche | Guide them to be more specific. Suggest uploading papers they already have |
| "The synthesis seems shallow" | Only a few sources found | Help them add more sources or adjust search filters |
| "I don't know what to do with the output" | Unfamiliar with the workspace model | Point them to the Getting Started guide. Show them editing and agent chat |
| "The agent didn't understand my question" | Vague prompt | Share examples of good agent prompts |
| "It takes too long to run" | Pipeline processing time expectations | Set expectations: 3-5 minutes for full synthesis. It's doing real analysis |
| "The evidence mapping seems wrong" | Genuine product issue | Thank them, log the bug, fix it. This is exactly why they're here |

## Red Flags After Onboarding

Watch for these in the first 2 weeks:

| Signal | What It Means | Action |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Never logged in after 7 days | Forgot, busy, or lost interest | Send personal nudge email. If no response in 3 days, mark as inactive |
| Logged in but never completed a project | Confused or didn't see value | Personal outreach: "What got in the way? How can I help?" |
| Completed project but never returned | First experience wasn't compelling enough | Ask directly: "What would have made you come back?" |
| Active but no feedback submitted | Using it but not engaged with the program | Remind gently. Consider if they're better suited as a regular user than a Founding Researcher |
