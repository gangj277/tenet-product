# Screening Criteria

> How to select the right 30-50 Founding Researchers from applicants.

---

## The Goal

We're not looking for the most researchers. We're looking for the **right** researchers — people whose feedback will be high-signal, whose workflows match our product's core use case, and who will actually use Lumen for real work.

---

## Must-Have Criteria (All 4 Required)

### 1. Active Research Workflow
The applicant is **currently** working on research that involves reading and synthesizing multiple papers.

**Green flags:**
- "I'm writing my literature review chapter"
- "I'm conducting a systematic review on X"
- "I'm surveying a new research area for my postdoc"
- "I'm preparing a meta-analysis"

**Red flags:**
- "I'm interested in AI tools" (curiosity, no active need)
- "I might use this someday" (no urgency)
- "I just graduated" (no active research workflow)

### 2. Specific Pain Point
They can articulate a **concrete frustration** with their current research synthesis workflow — not a generic complaint.

**Green flags:**
- "I spend 2 weeks reading papers and still can't articulate the consensus"
- "I keep finding contradictions and have no system for tracking them"
- "My notes are in 4 different tools and I can't connect them"
- "I missed a contradicting paper and a reviewer caught it"

**Red flags:**
- "Research is hard" (too vague)
- "I want AI to do my work" (wrong expectation)
- "I'm curious about new tools" (no pain)

### 3. Willingness to Give Feedback
They express genuine interest in shaping the product, not just using it for free.

**Green flags:**
- "I'd love to help build a tool like this"
- "I have strong opinions about how research tools should work"
- "Happy to share feedback regularly"
- Mentions specific feature ideas in the application

**Red flags:**
- Application answers are one word each
- "Just give me access" energy
- No mention of collaboration or feedback

### 4. Research Credibility
They are a real researcher — PhD student, postdoc, faculty, research consultant, or equivalent.

**How to verify:**
- Google their name + institution (most researchers have a web presence)
- Check if their research topic is specific and real
- LinkedIn/Twitter profile with research activity

**This is NOT about prestige.** A first-year PhD student with a real systematic review to write is better than a tenured professor who's "just curious."

---

## Bonus Criteria (Nice to Have)

| Criterion | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| Uses multiple tools currently (Elicit + Zotero + Docs) | Experienced with alternatives — feedback will be comparative |
| Active on Twitter/Reddit academic communities | Natural amplifier when they talk about Lumen |
| Working on a systematic review or meta-analysis | Highest-pain use case, most likely to use Lumen intensively |
| Conducts interdisciplinary research | Tests Lumen's cross-domain capabilities |
| Different time zone from existing cohort | Diversity of experience, not just one academic culture |

---

## Diversity of the Cohort

Aim for diversity across these dimensions:

### Research Stage
- 40% PhD students (mid-program, active lit review)
- 30% Postdocs / early-career researchers
- 20% Research consultants / analysts
- 10% Faculty or senior researchers

### Field Distribution
- Don't over-index on one field. Aim for 5+ disciplines:
  - Health/medical sciences
  - Social sciences
  - Computer science / AI
  - Environmental science
  - Education / psychology
  - Policy / economics

### Geography
- Aim for at least 3 regions: North America, Europe, Asia-Pacific
- Different academic cultures surface different product needs

---

## Scoring Rubric

Rate each application 1-5 on each Must-Have criterion:

| Criterion | 1 (Weak) | 3 (OK) | 5 (Strong) |
|-----------|----------|--------|------------|
| Active research | "Might do research someday" | "Working on a paper" | "Currently writing systematic review, 30+ papers deep" |
| Specific pain | "Research is hard" | "Reading papers takes long" | "I spent 3 weeks on extraction and still missed contradictions" |
| Feedback willingness | One-word answers | "Happy to help" | "I have specific ideas about how evidence tracking should work" |
| Research credibility | Can't verify | Has a university affiliation | Published, active profile, specific research topic |

**Threshold: Average score of 3.5+ across all 4 criteria.**

## Rejection Handling

Not everyone can be accepted. Handle rejections with respect:

### Rejection Email Template

```
Subject: Lumen Founding Researcher Program — Update

Hi [First name],

Thank you for applying to Lumen's Founding Researcher Program. We
received a strong set of applications and were genuinely impressed
by the research community's interest.

For this first cohort, we're keeping it very small (under 50 researchers)
to ensure we can give each participant the attention they deserve.
Unfortunately, we weren't able to include everyone.

Here's what I can offer:

1. WAITLIST: You're on our priority list for the next cohort
   (likely in [timeframe]).

2. EARLY ACCESS: When we open public access, you'll be among
   the first to get in — before the general public.

3. STAY IN TOUCH: I'll send occasional updates about Lumen's
   progress. Reply to this email anytime with questions.

Your research on [their topic] sounds genuinely interesting, and
I hope Lumen will be useful to you soon.

[Your name]
Founder, Lumen
```

**Key principle**: Every rejected applicant should feel respected and want to hear from you again. They're future users and potential advocates.
