# ForTIFAI: Fending Off Recursive Training Induced Failure for AI Models

**Authors:** Soheil Zibakhsh Shabgahi, Pedram Aghazadeh, Azalia Mirhosseini, Farinaz Koushanfar

**Affiliations:** UC San Diego, Stanford University

## Summary

This paper addresses the critical issue of "model collapse" in generative AI, a phenomenon where models degrade over successive training generations due to increasing reliance on synthetic data. The authors identify model overconfidence in self-generated data as a key driver of this collapse. To combat this, they propose a novel confidence-aware loss function called Truncated Cross Entropy (TCE). TCE downweights high-confidence predictions during training, effectively mitigating the degradation of model performance and diversity. The proposed method is model-agnostic, theoretically grounded, and empirically validated across various modalities, demonstrating a significant delay in model collapse.

## Key Claims

*   Repeated training on synthetic data leads to "model collapse," causing performance degradation in generative AI models.
*   A primary cause of model collapse is the overconfidence of generative models in their own synthetic outputs.
*   A new loss function, Truncated Cross Entropy (TCE), can mitigate model collapse by downweighting high-confidence predictions during training.
*   TCE is model-agnostic and can be easily implemented on top of standard Cross Entropy loss.
*   Empirical results show that TCE can extend the model's fidelity interval before collapse by more than 2.3x.
*   The proposed method generalizes across different generative model types, including Transformers, Variational Autoencoders, and Gaussian Mixture Models.

## Contribution to Research Frame

This source directly supports the inferred research frame by providing a concrete technical solution to the problem of model collapse driven by recursive synthetic training. It offers both a theoretical understanding of *why* collapse occurs (overconfidence, statistical approximation errors) and an empirical demonstration of a mitigation strategy (TCE).

*   **Supports "statistical approximation errors and variance loss":** The paper explicitly links model collapse to approximation errors and variance loss, particularly in the context of recursive training loops. Figure 2 and the mathematical intuition section detail how variance can collapse in a fully synthetic training loop.
*   **Supports "mathematically modeled":** The paper provides a mathematical formulation for TCE and analyzes the variance dynamics in a simplified Gaussian model to illustrate how modifying the training objective can counteract collapse.
*   **Supports "empirically delayed":** The core contribution is the empirical validation of TCE, showing it significantly delays model collapse across different model architectures and datasets. Figure 6 quantifies this delay, showing TCE increases the time to failure by 2.3x compared to standard Cross Entropy.
*   **Supports "strategic data hygiene and hybrid training regimes":** While this paper focuses on a loss function modification rather than explicit data hygiene, TCE acts as a form of "intelligent data weighting" by downplaying potentially problematic synthetic data points based on confidence. The experimental setup also simulates a hybrid regime by including a fixed amount of new human-written text at each stage.

This paper provides strong evidence for the technical feasibility of mitigating model collapse through targeted modifications to the training process, specifically within the loss function design.
