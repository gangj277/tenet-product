# Collapse or Thrive? Perils and Promises of Synthetic Data in a Self-Generating World

## Summary

This document explores the phenomenon of "model collapse" in generative AI, where recursive training on synthetic data leads to a degradation of model performance and diversity. The research aims to determine whether this collapse is an unavoidable mathematical consequence or a solvable engineering problem. By analyzing the relationship between synthetic data saturation and the preservation of original data distributions, the project will assess the effectiveness of strategies like data mixing and the introduction of new data to create a guide for sustainable model training.

## Key Claims

*   Recursive training on synthetic data can lead to a decline in generative model performance and diversity.
*   The phenomenon of model collapse may be an inherent mathematical certainty or a manageable engineering challenge.
*   Understanding the interplay between synthetic data saturation and the original data distribution is crucial.
*   Strategies such as data mixing and injecting "fresh" data can potentially mitigate model collapse.
*   The research seeks to provide a technical roadmap for sustainable model training in the context of self-generating data.
