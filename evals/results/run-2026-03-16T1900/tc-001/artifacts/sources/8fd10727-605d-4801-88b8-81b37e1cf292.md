# Model Collapse Demystified: The Case of Regression

**Publication:** (Not specified in the provided text)

## Summary

This paper provides a theoretical analysis of "model collapse," a phenomenon where generative models degrade in performance when trained recursively on their own synthetic data. The authors study this in the context of high-dimensional regression, deriving analytic formulas for test error. They demonstrate that iterative retraining on synthetic data leads to a multiplicative degradation in performance, analogous to existing findings in other models. The paper also proposes adaptive regularization as a strategy to mitigate this collapse and presents modified scaling laws that reveal new crossover phenomena.

## Key Claims

*   Model collapse occurs when models trained recursively on their own generated data experience performance degradation, eventually becoming useless.
*   The test error in a recursive training setting can be analytically characterized by the formula: $E_{\text{test}} = E_{\text{clean}} + \Delta \text{Bias} + n \times \text{Scaling}$.
*   The term "Scaling" in the test error formula quantifies the negative impact of synthetic data, dependent on parameters like feature covariance, sample size, and noise levels.
*   The term "$\Delta$ Bias" represents an increase in bias due to synthetic data generation, which can be mitigated if the initial data generation process uses a sufficiently large sample size ($T_0 \ge d$).
*   In the special case of power-law spectra, new scaling laws are derived that quantitatively highlight the detrimental effects of training on synthetic data.
*   An optimal ridge regularization parameter can be determined to correct classical theory and manage the effects of training on fake data, potentially preventing catastrophic failure.
*   In the absence of label noise, but with an under-parameterized fake data generator, the increase in bias ($\Delta$ Bias) can be a positive constant that does not grow with the number of generations, due to the generator's subspace not containing the ground truth model.
