# The Curse of Recursion: Training on Generated Data Makes Models Forget

**Publication:** (Not specified in the provided text)

This paper introduces and defines the phenomenon of "model collapse," a degenerative process where generative models trained on data produced by previous generations of models suffer irreversible defects. This occurs because the generated data pollutes the training set for subsequent models, causing them to forget the original data distribution, particularly its "tails" (low-probability events). The authors demonstrate this phenomenon across various model types, including Gaussian Mixture Models, Variational Autoencoders, and Large Language Models, attributing it to compounding statistical and functional approximation errors. They conclude that access to genuine, human-produced data is crucial for sustaining the benefits of large-scale data training and that data about human interactions with AI systems will become increasingly valuable.

## Key Claims

*   Training generative models on data produced by previous generations leads to "model collapse," a degradation of the model's ability to represent the original data distribution.
*   Model collapse is characterized by the disappearance of the "tails" of the data distribution and can eventually lead to models converging to a low-variance point estimate.
*   The phenomenon is observed across various generative model types, including GMMs, VAEs, and LLMs.
*   Two primary causes for model collapse are identified: statistical approximation error (due to finite samples) and functional approximation error (due to model expressiveness limitations).
*   Model collapse is an inevitable process in recursive training loops unless mitigated.
*   Access to genuine, human-produced data is essential to counteract model collapse and maintain the integrity of learned distributions.

## Relevant Evidence/Quotes

*   "We find that use of model-generated content in training causes irreversible defects in the resulting models, where tails of the original content distribution disappear. We refer to this effect as model collapse."
*   "We discover that learning from data produced by other models causes model collapse – a degenerative process whereby, over time, models forget the true underlying data distribution, even in the absence of a shift in the distribution over time."
*   "In early model collapse the model begins losing information about the tails of the distribution; in the late model collapse model entangles different modes of the original distributions and converges to a distribution that carries little resemblance to the original one, often with very small variance."
*   "The value of data collected about genuine human interactions with systems will be increasingly valuable in the presence of content generated by LLMs in data crawled from the Internet."
*   **Example of text outputs of an OPT-125m model affected by Model Collapse:** The provided example shows how text generation degrades significantly over generations (Gen 0 to Gen 9), losing coherence and becoming repetitive or nonsensical.
