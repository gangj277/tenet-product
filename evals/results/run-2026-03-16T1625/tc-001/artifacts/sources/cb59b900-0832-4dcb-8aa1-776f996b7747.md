# Document Metadata

*   **Title:** The Probabilistic Foundations of Surveillance Failure: From False Alerts to Structural Bias
*   **Author:** Marco Pollanen
*   **Publication Info:** *Mathematics*, 14(1), 49 (2026); originally submitted to arXiv:2511.12459 [stat.ME] (Nov/Dec 2025).
*   **Subject Areas:** Statistics (Methodology), Computers and Society, Probability.

---

### Summary
This paper establishes a mathematical framework for understanding why high-dimensional screening and surveillance systems inevitably fail as they scale. Pollanen argues that while matching a specific set of attributes is statistically rare, matching *any* subset of attributes (combinatorial matching) leads to a "sharp transition" where false alerts become a mathematical certainty. The research demonstrates that beyond a critical threshold of data scale and attribute correlation, systems lose their evidential value entirely. This failure is structural, meaning it cannot be corrected by better algorithms or tighter thresholds, and it inherently produces "structural bias" when surveillance exposure is unequal across populations.

---

### Key Claims
*   **Combinatorial Explosion of False Positives:** In high-dimensional spaces (e.g., 1,000 attributes), the probability of an innocent person matching a *specific* set of criteria is near zero, but the probability of matching *any* subset of those criteria (e.g., 15 out of 1,000) is nearly 100% at scale.
*   **The "Sharp Transition" to Unreliability:** Surveillance systems do not degrade linearly; they undergo a phase transition from reliable to unreliable as data scale or the number of monitored attributes increases.
*   **Inevitability of Structural Bias:** Because these failures are probabilistic and tied to "surveillance exposure," groups subjected to more frequent data collection will suffer higher rates of false alerts as a mathematical necessity, regardless of the algorithm's intent.
*   **Limits of Refinement:** Beyond a "critical scale," the loss of evidential value is a structural property of the high-dimensional space that cannot be mitigated by "algorithmic refinement" or "threshold adjustment."
*   **Correlation Collapses Dimensionality:** As data points become more correlated, the "effective dimensionality" of the system collapses, accelerating the transition into a regime where alerts carry no meaningful information.

---

### Relevant Evidence & Quotes
*   **The Combinatorial Trap:** "Consider a system that monitors 1,000 attributes... Matching 15 pre-specified attributes has probability $10^{-35}$... But operational systems... might flag anyone who matches *any* 15 of the 1,000. In a city of one million innocent people, this produces about 226 false alerts."
*   **Structural Failure:** "These limits are structural: beyond a critical scale, failure cannot be prevented through threshold adjustment or algorithmic refinement."
*   **Loss of Evidential Value:** Systems enter regimes where "alerts lose evidential value even when individual coincidences remain vanishingly rare."
*   **Mathematical Inevitability of Bias:** "Unequal surveillance exposures magnify failure, making 'structural bias' mathematically inevitable."

### Contribution to Research Frame: "Managed Degradation"

This source provides a **foundational mathematical warning** that challenges the "Managed Degradation" frame by suggesting that some forms of system collapse are **inherent laws of high-dimensional probability** rather than mere technical hurdles.

*   **Challenges Mitigation Strategies:** The research frame asks if "data cleaning" or "threshold tuning" can prevent collapse. Pollanen’s findings suggest that once a system passes a "critical scale" of data/attributes, these refinements are mathematically incapable of preventing the loss of evidential value.
*   **Parallel to Model Collapse:** While the paper focuses on surveillance, its findings on "effective dimensionality" and "sharp transitions" mirror the mechanics of model collapse. Just as surveillance loses meaning when "any match" becomes likely, generative models lose meaning when they converge on the most probable (but non-representative) outputs.
*   **Structural vs. Technical:** The paper shifts the focus from *implementation flaws* (which can be fixed) to *mathematical consequences* (which cannot). This supports the "theoretical proofs of collapse" side of the research project, suggesting that recursive training on high-dimensional synthetic data may hit a "probabilistic limit" where the output inevitably loses its link to the original distribution's "truth."
*   **Data-to-Noise Ratios:** It reinforces the subquestion regarding "critical ratios," suggesting there is a mathematical point of no return where the accumulation of coincidental (or synthetic) noise overwhelms the signal.
