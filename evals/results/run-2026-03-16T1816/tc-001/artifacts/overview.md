# Project Overview: Model Collapse From Recursive Synthetic Data Training

### 1. Original Research Question
Under what conditions does recursive training on synthetic data lead to model collapse in generative models?

### 2. Interpreted Research Objective
This research aims to determine the specific technical thresholds and data-mixing ratios that trigger or prevent model collapse. The objective is to move beyond the "doom" narrative to identify actionable architectural and data-curation safeguards, distinguishing between mathematical inevitability and manageable engineering challenges.

### 3. Inferred Research Frame
The working hypothesis is that model collapse is not an absolute law of scaling but a function of data-to-noise ratios and distribution drift. It is hypothesized that collapse can be mathematically modeled and empirically mitigated through strategic data hygiene, specifically by prioritizing data accumulation over replacement and maintaining a threshold of "fresh" real-world data.

### 4. Scope Boundaries
*   **In-Scope:** Machine learning literature focusing on recursive training, synthetic data reuse, distribution degradation, and the mathematical mechanisms of variance loss and entropy decay.
*   **Out-of-Scope:** Product-level commentary, non-technical blog posts, and speculative industry opinion pieces.
*   **Temporal Focus:** 2023–2025.
*   **Geographic Focus:** Global academic and industrial research.
