# AI Is Eating Itself to Death — and Nobody Is Stopping It

**Publication:** AI Advances (Medium)
**Author:** Delanoe Pirard
**Date:** February 11, 2026

## Summary

This article introduces the concept of "model collapse," a phenomenon where AI models degrade over successive generations of training on synthetic data. Drawing an analogy to Frederic Bartlett's 1932 "Telephone Game" experiment, the author explains how information, particularly rare or unique elements, is lost with each reproduction. Recent research published in Nature (Shumailov et al., 2024) has demonstrated that AI models exhibit a similar, progressive, and irreversible degradation when trained on data generated by previous AI models. This "eating itself to death" implies a fundamental challenge for the long-term sustainability and quality of AI development if not addressed.

## Key Claims

*   Model collapse is a progressive and irreversible degradation of AI models trained on synthetic data.
*   This phenomenon is analogous to Frederic Bartlett's "Telephone Game" experiment, where information is lost and generalized over successive reproductions.
*   Rare information and unique characteristics are the first to disappear in the process of model collapse.
*   AI models, when trained recursively on synthetic data, exhibit a similar degradation pattern, losing what made the original data unique.
*   The discovery of model collapse in AI is a recent finding, highlighted by research published in Nature in 2024.

## Relevant Evidence/Quotes

*   "Nine mirrors. Nine generations. Each reflection loses what made the original unique. By the ninth, only noise remains. This is model collapse."
*   "By the seventh reproduction, the story was unrecognizable. The spirits and canoes had vanished. The elements culturally foreign to the British participants had been replaced by bland generalizations. The tale had been 'normalized,' stripped of everything that made it unique."
*   "The takeaway: each link in the chain preserves what feels familiar and discards the rest. Rare information disappears first."
*   "In 2024, a team of researchers from Cambridge, Toronto and Oxford published a disturbing discovery in Nature : artificial intelligence models do exactly the same thing ( Shumailov et al., Nature 2024 ). They called it model collapse : a progressive, irreversible degradation of AI models trained on synthetic data."
