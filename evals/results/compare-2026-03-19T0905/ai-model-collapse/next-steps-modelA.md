# Next Steps: Model Collapse Research Project

The current research confirms that model collapse is a mathematical certainty in closed-loop, fully synthetic training environments. The focus must now shift from "if it happens" to "how to quantify the decay rate" and "what are the viable, non-prohibitive mitigation strategies."

## 1. Concrete Follow-up Research Directions

*   **Modality-Specific Sensitivity Analysis:** Compare the "collapse velocity" of text-based LLMs versus diffusion-based image models. Investigate if the discrete nature of tokenization in LLMs accelerates the "tail-cutting" effect compared to the continuous latent space of image generation.
*   **The "Watermark-Filter" Pipeline:** Evaluate the feasibility of using cryptographic or statistical watermarking as a primary filter for synthetic data. Research whether training a "synthetic-data detector" classifier is a viable, scalable intervention to maintain a high-fidelity training set.
*   **Data Diversity Metrics:** Move beyond Perplexity and FID. Investigate the use of **V-measure** or **Entropy-based metrics** to detect "early-stage" collapse (before it is visible in downstream task performance).

## 2. Promising Hypothesis Refinements

*   **The "Hybrid-Stability" Hypothesis:** Instead of a fixed ratio, the stability of a model is a function of the *information entropy* of the synthetic data relative to the real data. If synthetic data is filtered for high-entropy/high-diversity samples, the "exponentially smaller" requirement for real data may be relaxed.
*   **Architectural Resilience:** Hypothesize that architectures with explicit latent space constraints or those utilizing "Retrieval-Augmented Generation" (RAG) are more resilient to collapse because they decouple the generation process from the static weights of the model.
