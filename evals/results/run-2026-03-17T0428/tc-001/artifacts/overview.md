# Project Overview: Model Collapse From Recursive Synthetic Data Training

### 1. Research Question
Under what conditions does recursive training on synthetic data lead to model collapse in generative models?

### 2. Research Objective
This project synthesizes current machine learning literature to determine the technical boundaries and mitigation feasibility of recursive training loops. The objective is to identify the "tipping point" where synthetic data quality and volume cause irreversible model degradation, distinguishing between inevitable mathematical decay and manageable engineering challenges.

### 3. Research Frame
The working hypothesis is that model collapse is a conditional failure mode driven by the loss of statistical diversity and the narrowing of probability distributions. While recursive training on synthetic data poses a significant risk, the phenomenon is not necessarily an inevitable outcome; it can be delayed or mitigated through strategic data curation, hybrid training regimes (mixing real and synthetic data), and algorithmic regularization.

### 4. Scope Boundaries
*   **In-Scope:** Machine learning literature concerning recursive training, synthetic data reuse, distribution degradation, and statistical modeling of model collapse.
*   **Out-of-Scope:** Product-level commentary, non-technical blog posts, and general industry speculation regarding AI adoption.
*   **Time Horizon:** 2023–2025.
*   **Geography:** Global academic and research literature.
