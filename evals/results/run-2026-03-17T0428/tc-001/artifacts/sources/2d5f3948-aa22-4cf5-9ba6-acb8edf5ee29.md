# The Curse of Recursion: Training on Generated Data Makes Models Forget

**Publication Info:** Not specified in the provided text.

## Summary

This paper introduces and defines "model collapse," a phenomenon where generative models trained on data produced by previous generations of models degrade over time. This degradation occurs because the generated data eventually pollutes the training set, causing subsequent models to misperceive the original data distribution. The authors demonstrate this collapse in various model types, including Gaussian Mixture Models, Variational Autoencoders, and Large Language Models (LLMs). They identify two primary causes: statistical approximation error (due to finite sampling) and functional approximation error (due to model expressiveness limitations). The paper argues that to sustain the benefits of large-scale data, access to genuine human-produced data is crucial, as it is essential for learning the tails of the underlying distribution.

## Key Claims

*   Training generative models on data produced by previous generations of models leads to "model collapse," a degenerative process.
*   Model collapse results in models forgetting the true underlying data distribution, with tails of the distribution disappearing first.
*   The phenomenon is observed across various generative model types, including GMMs, VAEs, and LLMs.
*   The primary causes of model collapse are statistical approximation error (finite samples) and functional approximation error (model limitations).
*   Access to genuine human-produced data is essential to counteract model collapse and maintain the value of large-scale datasets.

## Relevant Evidence/Quotes

*   "We find that use of model-generated content in training causes irreversible defects in the resulting models, where tails of the original content distribution disappear."
*   "We refer to this effect as model collapse... and show that it can occur in Variational Autoencoders, Gaussian Mixture Models, and LLMs."
*   "We discover that learning from data produced by other models causes model collapse – a degenerative process whereby, over time, models forget the true underlying data distribution, even in the absence of a shift in the distribution over time."
*   "The value of data collected about genuine human interactions with systems will be increasingly valuable in the presence of content generated by LLMs in data crawled from the Internet."
*   **Causes of Model Collapse:**
    *   **Statistical approximation error:** "this is the primary type of error, which arises due to the number of samples being finite, and disappears as the number of samples tends to infinity."
    *   **Functional approximation error:** "this is a secondary type of error, which stems from our function approximators being insufficiently expressive..."
