# ForTIFAI: Fending Off Recursive Training Induced Failure for AI Models

**Authors:** Soheil Zibakhsh Shabgahi, Pedram Aghazadeh, Azalia Mirhosseini, Farinaz Koushanfar

**Affiliations:** UC San Diego, Stanford University

**Abstract:** This paper addresses the critical issue of "model collapse" in generative AI, a phenomenon where models trained recursively on their own synthetic outputs degrade in performance over time. The authors identify model overconfidence in self-generated data as a key driver of this collapse. They propose a novel confidence-aware loss function called Truncated Cross Entropy (TCE) that downweights high-confidence predictions during training. TCE is demonstrated to significantly delay model collapse, extending the model's fidelity interval by over 2.3x. The proposed method is model-agnostic and effective across different modalities.

## Key Claims

*   Generative AI models trained recursively on synthetic data suffer from "model collapse," leading to performance degradation.
*   A primary cause of model collapse is the overconfidence of models in their self-generated data.
*   A new loss function, Truncated Cross Entropy (TCE), mitigates model collapse by downweighting high-confidence predictions.
*   TCE is model-agnostic and can be easily implemented on top of standard Cross Entropy loss.
*   Empirical results show TCE extends the model's fidelity interval by more than 2.3x before collapse.
*   The proposed method is effective across various generative models and modalities.

## Contribution to Research Frame

This source directly supports the inferred research frame by providing a concrete technical solution to mitigate model collapse, which is framed as a conditional failure mode.

*   **Supports "conditional failure mode":** The paper demonstrates that model collapse is not an inevitable outcome but can be significantly delayed and managed through specific training strategies. The proposed TCE loss function acts as a mechanism to maintain distributional stability.
*   **Supports "loss of statistical diversity":** The paper's mathematical analysis and empirical results (e.g., Figure 7 showing lower KL divergence with TCE) directly address the loss of statistical diversity as a core problem. The overconfidence mechanism and the proposed solution of downweighting high-confidence predictions are aimed at preserving this diversity.
*   **Supports "theoretically modeled":** The paper provides a theoretical grounding for the problem by analyzing the covariance collapse in synthetic training loops and deriving a variance amplification factor for truncated distributions.
*   **Supports "empirically delayed":** The core contribution is the empirical validation of TCE, showing a 2.3x increase in the time to failure and improved performance across various benchmarks, thus demonstrating that collapse can be empirically delayed.
*   **Supports "strategic data curation and hybrid training regimes":** While the paper focuses on loss function modification, the experimental setup (Section 3) simulates a hybrid training regime by progressively adding synthetic data alongside a fixed amount of new "real" data, aligning with the frame's emphasis on data management. The loss function itself acts as a strategic element within the training process.
