# Model Collapse Demystified: The Case of Regression

**Publication Info:** Not specified in the provided text.

## Summary

This paper provides a theoretical analysis of "model collapse" in high-dimensional kernel regression, a phenomenon where model performance degrades due to iterative training on its own generated data. The authors derive analytic formulae to quantify this degradation under various conditions, including polynomial decaying spectral and source conditions. They identify modified scaling laws that exhibit new crossover phenomena and propose an adaptive regularization strategy to mitigate model collapse, which is supported by theoretical results and experimental validation.

## Key Claims

*   Model collapse is a performance degradation in generative AI models caused by recursive training on AI-generated data.
*   The paper offers a theoretical framework for analyzing model collapse using high-dimensional kernel regression.
*   Analytic formulae are derived to precisely describe test error under iterative retraining on synthetic data.
*   Model collapse modifies standard scaling laws, leading to a multiplicative degradation of performance.
*   An adaptive regularization strategy can be employed to mitigate the effects of model collapse.
