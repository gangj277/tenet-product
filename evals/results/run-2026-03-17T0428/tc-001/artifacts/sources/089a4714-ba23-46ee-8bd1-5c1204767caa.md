# Is Model Collapse Inevitable? Breaking the Curse of Recursion by Accumulating Real and Synthetic Data

**Publication:** arXiv e-prints
**Date:** April 2024
**DOI:** 10.48550/arXiv.2404.01413

## Summary

This paper investigates the phenomenon of "model collapse," where generative models trained on their own synthetic outputs progressively degrade in performance. Previous research often assumed that new synthetic data replaces old real data, leading to collapse. However, this study explores a more realistic scenario where data accumulates over time. The authors empirically demonstrate that accumulating synthetic data alongside original real data effectively prevents model collapse across various model types (language, molecule generation, image generation) and configurations. They further provide a theoretical framework, extending prior work, to explain that accumulating data leads to a finite upper bound on test error, thus avoiding collapse.

## Key Claims

*   Training generative models on their own synthetic outputs can lead to "model collapse," a progressive degradation of performance.
*   Previous studies on model collapse often assumed data replacement, which is less realistic than data accumulation.
*   Accumulating successive generations of synthetic data alongside the original real data effectively avoids model collapse.
*   This avoidance of model collapse through data accumulation holds true across different model sizes, architectures, hyperparameters, and data types (text, molecules, images).
*   A theoretical framework shows that data accumulation results in a finite upper bound on test error, independent of the number of training iterations, thus preventing collapse.

## Relevant Evidence/Quotes

*   "we ask: what effect does accumulating data have on model collapse?"
*   "We confirm that replacing the original real data by each generation's synthetic data does indeed tend towards model collapse, then demonstrate that accumulating the successive generations of synthetic data alongside the original real data avoids model collapse"
*   "these results hold across a range of model sizes, architectures, and hyperparameters."
*   "we extend this argument to prove that if data instead accumulate, the test error has a finite upper bound independent of the number of iterations, meaning model collapse no longer occurs."

## Contribution to Research Frame

This source directly addresses the core concern of the research frame regarding the technical boundaries of recursive training loops and the potential for model collapse. It provides strong empirical evidence and theoretical backing for the idea that model collapse is **conditional rather than inevitable**, specifically demonstrating that the **ratio of real-to-synthetic data** and the **preservation of original data distributions** (through accumulation) are key factors in mitigation.

The paper's findings align with the frame's assumption that collapse is a manageable engineering challenge, not a mathematical certainty. It offers a concrete mitigation strategy (data accumulation) that directly counters the "loss of statistical diversity" mechanism. The study's focus on empirical results across different domains strengthens the argument that this is a generalizable phenomenon and solution. It contributes by offering a robust counter-argument to the inevitability of collapse and a practical method for its avoidance, which can inform the evaluation of other mitigation strategies.
