# A Tale of Tails: Model Collapse as a Change of Scaling Laws

**Publication:** Not specified in the provided text.

This paper introduces a theoretical framework and empirical validation for understanding "model collapse," a phenomenon where AI models degrade in performance due to training on synthetic data. The authors propose that synthetic data, often generated with truncated or narrowed statistical tails compared to natural data, disrupts established neural scaling laws. This disruption leads to a loss of performance scaling, skill un-learning, and can even result in grokking when clean human data is mixed with synthetic data. The research offers new scaling laws to explain these effects and suggests data mixing as a mitigation strategy.

## Key Claims:

*   Synthetic data, due to its often truncated or narrowed statistical tails, fundamentally alters the scaling laws that govern model performance.
*   Model collapse is characterized by a loss of performance scaling, shifted scaling across generations of AI-generated data, and the un-learning of specific skills.
*   The paper introduces new "Double Scaling Laws" and "Triplet Scaling Laws" to mathematically describe model collapse in simplified and memory-limited models, respectively.
*   Recursive generation of AI data leads to multiplicative degradation in scaling laws with each generation.
*   Mixing a small amount of clean, human-generated data with synthetic data can mitigate model collapse and induce a "grokking" phenomenon where performance initially plateaus before improving.
