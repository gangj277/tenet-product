# Next Steps: Model Collapse Research

This document outlines the immediate research priorities to transition from a theoretical understanding of model collapse to actionable engineering thresholds and mitigation strategies.

## 1. Concrete Follow-up Research Directions

*   **Quantifying the "Critical Ratio" for Multi-Modal Data:** Conduct a comparative analysis between text-based LLMs and Diffusion models to determine if the "real-to-synthetic" ratio required for stability differs by modality. Current evidence suggests discrete distributions (text) may have "absorbing states" that accelerate collapse compared to continuous distributions (images).
*   **The "Accumulation vs. Subsampling" Trade-off:** Investigate the performance delta between "Full Accumulation" (keeping all real and synthetic data) and "Fixed-Budget Subsampling" (maintaining a constant dataset size by replacing old data). Determine the mathematical point where subsampling leads to a performance plateau.
*   **Tail-Preservation Benchmarking:** Develop a metric specifically for "Tail Diversity" (e.g., rare token frequency or out-of-distribution robustness) rather than just perplexity or FID scores. Use this to measure the "Tail Truncation" effect across 5+ generations of recursive training.
*   **Impact of Model Capacity on Collapse Speed:** Test the hypothesis that larger models (higher parameter counts) are more resilient to synthetic noise. Determine if "Functional Approximation Error" decreases significantly as model capacity increases, thereby delaying collapse.

## 2. Promising Hypothesis Refinements

*   **Refinement 1 (The Accumulation Hypothesis):** Model collapse is not an inevitability but a consequence of the *Data Replacement* paradigm. If a "Real Data Anchor" (at least 10% of the total training set) is maintained and synthetic data is *accumulated* rather than used to replace real data, the error remains bounded.
*   **Refinement 2 (The Self-Verification Hypothesis):** Fully synthetic training loops can remain stable *if and only if* a high-precision filtering mechanism (e.g., a "Judge" model or a verification loss) is applied to the synthetic data before it is re-ingested.
*   **Refinement 3 (The Regularization Paradox):** Standard weight decay and dropout settings optimized for real data are sub-optimal for synthetic mixtures. Stability requires *adaptive regularization* that accounts for the reduced variance in synthetic distributions.

### Methods & Datasets
*   **Method: Self-Verification Filtering:** Investigate the use of "Confidence-Based Filtering" or "Consistency Checks" (e.g., Self-Consistency in CoT) as a pre-processing step for synthetic data.
*   **Method: Adaptive Regularization:** Look for implementations of "Noise-Aware Loss Functions" that weigh synthetic samples lower than real samples during the gradient update.
*   **Dataset: TinyStories or SlimPajama:** Use these as controlled environments to simulate 10+ generations of recursive training at a lower compute cost to validate the "Tail Truncation" metrics.
