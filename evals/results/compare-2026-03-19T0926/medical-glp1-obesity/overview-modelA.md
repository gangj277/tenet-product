# Research Overview: GLP-1 Therapies for Obesity

### 1. Research Question
What does recent literature say about the efficacy and safety of GLP-1-based therapies for obesity management in adults without diabetes?

### 2. Research Objective
This project provides a clinically grounded synthesis of the weight-loss efficacy, cardiometabolic benefits, and safety tradeoffs of GLP-1 receptor agonists (GLP-1 RAs) and dual agonists. The objective is to evaluate the comparative clinical value of semaglutide and tirzepatide, specifically identifying whether superior weight loss outcomes are balanced by gastrointestinal tolerability issues and the necessity for chronic administration.

### 3. Inferred Research Frame
The project operates under the **"Efficacy-Tolerability Tradeoff"** hypothesis. This frame posits that while GLP-1-based therapies represent a transformative intervention for obesity, their real-world clinical utility is constrained by dose-dependent gastrointestinal adverse events, high attrition rates, and the physiological requirement for long-term maintenance to prevent weight regain.

### 4. Scope Boundaries
*   **Population:** Adults with obesity or overweight, specifically excluding those with type 2 diabetes where possible to isolate efficacy in non-diabetic cohorts.
*   **Interventions:** Semaglutide (2.4 mg) and Tirzepatide (various doses).
*   **Evidence Type:** Systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and pivotal Phase III clinical trials.
*   **Time Horizon:** 2022–2025.
*   **Geography:** Global literature.
