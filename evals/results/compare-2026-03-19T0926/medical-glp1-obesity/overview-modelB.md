# GLP-1 Therapies for Obesity — Project Overview

## 1) Original research question
**What does recent literature say about the efficacy and safety of GLP-1-based therapies for obesity management in adults without diabetes?**

## 2) Interpreted research objective
To produce a **clinically grounded synthesis** of:
- **Weight-loss efficacy** of GLP-1–based therapies in **adults without diabetes**, with emphasis on the **comparative** profiles of **semaglutide vs tirzepatide**.
- **Cardiometabolic benefits** (e.g., blood pressure, inflammatory markers, lipids), including whether effects occur **independently of weight loss**.
- **Safety tradeoffs** and clinician-relevant evidence gaps, focusing on **adverse event burden**, discontinuation, and uncertainty around **long-term outcomes and weight maintenance**.

## 3) Inferred research frame / working hypothesis
**Working hypothesis (under examination):**  
GLP-1-based therapies show **clinically meaningful weight loss** and **cardiometabolic improvement**, but **adverse effects** and **study heterogeneity** significantly affect interpretation of net benefit.

**Working frame used for analysis:** **“Efficacy–Tolerability Tradeoff.”**  
- **Efficacy:** dose-dependent reduction in body weight and improved cardiometabolic markers over typical trial durations (~68–72 weeks).
- **Tolerability:** gastrointestinal adverse events are the dominant limiting factor and may drive discontinuation.
- **Clinical durability:** obesity pharmacotherapy likely requires **chronic administration**, with weight regain reported after withdrawal.
- **Comparative uncertainty:** differences between semaglutide and tirzepatide must be interpreted in light of variability in trial design, baseline characteristics, and attrition.

## 4) Scope boundaries
- **Population:** **Adults with obesity** (or overweight with obesity-related comorbidity where included), **excluding diabetes** where possible.
- **Interventions:** GLP-1–based therapies for weight management, with priority on:
  - **Semaglutide** (commonly 2.4 mg formulation in obesity trials)
  - **Tirzepatide** (dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist)
- **Evidence type prioritization:** Systematic reviews and meta-analyses are prioritized for synthesis; primary RCTs may be considered where directly needed to contextualize outcomes.
- **Outcomes of interest (must answer):**
  1. **Magnitude of weight loss** (mean change; and proportions achieving clinically meaningful thresholds when reported).
  2. **Cardiovascular/cardiometabolic benefits** (e.g., BP, lipids, CRP; and evidence regarding CV outcomes where available).
  3. **Safety issues / evidence gaps** relevant to clinical decision-making.
- **Time horizon:** Focus on literature published **2022–2025** (as reflected in the included sources).
- **Geography:** **Global** literature.
- **Notable exclusion/limitation boundary (implicit):** Real-world effectiveness and long-term (3+ years) outcomes may be less completely captured in the available high-level summaries; these are treated explicitly as **evidence gaps** rather than assumed facts.
