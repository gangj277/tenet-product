## Claim 1 (strongest): Semaglutide 2.4 mg and tirzepatide produce large, clinically meaningful weight loss in adults with obesity/overweight without diabetes (vs placebo) over ~68–72 weeks
- **Claim statement:** Semaglutide 2.4 mg and tirzepatide (particularly higher doses) achieve superior weight loss versus placebo in non-diabetic adults with obesity/overweight over roughly 68–72 weeks.
- **Supporting sources:**
  - Semaglutide 2.4 mg reduced body weight by **-14.9% vs -2.4% with placebo** after **68 weeks** in phase III trials (non-diabetic populations). [Source: 500c72dd-825a-4964-9627-d776aaf05dad]
  - Tirzepatide reduced body weight by **15% (5 mg), 19.5% (10 mg), and 20.9% (15 mg) vs 3.1% with placebo** at **72 weeks** in people with obesity but **without diabetes**. [Source: a497e563-aa2f-4ee1-9430-db018301bcbb]
- **Contradicting sources:** None identified in the consolidated findings.
- **Confidence assessment:** **High** — effect sizes and directionality are consistent across high-level synthesis statements and are anchored to phase III–level comparisons (vs placebo) in non-diabetic cohorts.

## Claim 2: Gastrointestinal adverse events are the key tolerability limitation for GLP-1-based therapies, are dose-related, and contribute to reduced adherence/discontinuation
- **Claim statement:** Nausea and diarrhea occur significantly more often with GLP-1 therapies (including semaglutide), show dose-dependency, and are a major driver of tolerability limits.
- **Supporting sources:**
  - Semaglutide: nausea and diarrhea occur significantly more frequently than placebo (**nausea RR 2.58; diarrhea RR 2.01**) with subgroup evidence suggesting **dose-dependent** effects. [Source: 567d18dd-0fc4-4773-a3bf-d531739526a0]
  - GLP-1 agents: **dose-dependent gastrointestinal effects limit the therapeutic index**, motivating development of alternative pharmacology. [Source: da8c7eae-b56f-42dc-be6e-ebbeed9d5c87]
- **Contradicting sources:** None identified in the consolidated findings.
- **Confidence assessment:** **High** — the claim is supported by quantified adverse-event comparisons and is consistent with mechanistic/clinical summaries emphasizing GI tolerability as the limiting factor.
