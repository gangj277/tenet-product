## next-steps.md — GLP-1 Therapies for Obesity (Adults w/o Diabetes)

### 1) Concrete follow-up research directions (actionable)

#### A. Quantify *head-to-head* and *comparative* weight-loss efficacy (semaglutide vs tirzepatide)
1. **Run/collect a dose-stratified comparison** using either:
   - a **network meta-analysis** (NMA) that includes semaglutide 2.4 mg and tirzepatide doses (5/10/15 mg), or
   - a **two-step extraction** approach: extract mean % weight change and responder rates from each key RCT, then compute a standardized comparative effect (e.g., difference in mean % change and odds ratios for ≥10% / ≥15% loss).
2. **Subgroup extraction** to reduce heterogeneity:
   - baseline BMI strata (e.g., <35 vs ≥35)
   - age and sex (if reported)
   - weight-loss target definitions (all-cause vs completer analyses)
3. Output to write:
   - effect size table by dose and endpoint (68–72 weeks)
   - responder distribution (≥5/10/15/20% thresholds) with absolute risks if available.

#### B. Update cardiometabolic benefit estimates in *non-diabetes*
1. Identify meta-analyses (2022–2025) reporting **BP, lipids, inflammation**, and ideally **insulin resistance indices** (e.g., HOMA-IR), even if HbA1c is not central.
2. Extract and report **absolute changes** where possible:
   - SBP/DBP (mmHg)
   - LDL-C, HDL-C, TG (mg/dL or mmol/L)
   - hs-CRP / CRP
   - waist circumference and/or visceral fat measures (if included)
3. Separate:
   - **weight-mediated** effects vs **independent** effects (if authors adjusted for weight change).

#### C. Safety deep-dive: adverse events that matter clinically beyond “GI”
1. Build a safety evidence matrix focused on:
   - **GI events** (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation) and **treatment discontinuation due to AEs**
   - **gallbladder/biliary disease** events (cholelithiasis, cholecystitis, biliary colic)
   - **pancreatitis** (and how studies adjudicated/defined it)
   - **kidney outcomes** (AKI, dehydration-related events)
   - **injection-site reactions** and **hypersensitivity**
   - **cardiac safety signals** (e.g., heart rate changes, arrhythmia signals if captured)
2. If existing meta-analyses are available, prefer those that report:
   - **RR/OR for grade ≥3 AEs**
   - **rates of serious adverse events (SAEs)** and discontinuation.
3. If not:
   - pivot to extracting safety endpoints from the largest non-diabetes RCTs and reconstruct event rates.

#### D. Long-term evidence: 3+ year outcomes and weight maintenance (especially tirzepatide)
1. Search specifically for:
   - **extension studies** (e.g., 2-year/3-year extension phases)
   - trials with **treatment continuation vs withdrawal** designs
2. Extract:
   - mean % weight change at 2, 3, and (if available) 4 years
   - proportion regaining weight after discontinuation
   - long-term safety rates (including biliary, pancreatitis adjudication)
3. Deliverable: a “**short-term vs long-term**” comparison chart for semaglutide and tirzepatide in non-diabetic populations.

#### E. Lean mass and body composition (fat vs muscle loss)
1. Identify RCTs and/or secondary analyses that include **DEXA** or similar composition endpoints.
2. Extract:
   - fat mass change vs lean mass change
   - whether protocols included resistance training counseling and whether that affected lean retention
3. If evidence is sparse for tirzepatide in non-diabetes, explicitly quantify the evidence gap and recommend targeted extraction from available extensions or mechanistic studies.

#### F. Real-world evidence (RWE) to complement RCTs
1. Look for large claims/EHR studies (2022–2025) in **obesity without diabetes**:
   - discontinuation patterns
   - rates of GI events leading to discontinuation
   - gallbladder/biliary and pancreatitis incidence in routine care
2. Extract confounder handling methods (propensity scores, comparator definitions).
3. Use RWE primarily to estimate *absolute risks* and *persistence/attrition*, not to replace RCT efficacy estimates.

### 2) Promising hypothesis refinements (based on what the evidence suggests)

1. **Tolerability as a mediator of real-world efficacy**
   - Refine the hypothesis: *the clinically meaningful weight loss seen in RCTs may translate less completely into real-world effectiveness because discontinuation and dose intolerance disproportionately affect higher-risk patients* (older age, higher baseline BMI, history of GI disorders).
2. **Dose-response may differ between semaglutide and tirzepatide for GI burden**
   - Refine: *tirzepatide’s superior weight loss may partially be “bought” with greater GI intolerance at higher doses, but its tolerability curve could be flatter in some subgroups; comparative GI discontinuation rates will clarify this.*
3. **Cardiometabolic improvements might be partially independent of weight**
   - Refine: *reductions in CRP and BP appear consistent across analyses, suggesting effects beyond weight alone; confirm by extracting studies that report mediation-adjusted outcomes or by comparing changes relative to weight change.*
4. **Long-term weight maintenance is the key uncertainty**
   - Refine: *short-term efficacy is robust, but the critical question for clinical utility is trajectory after 1–3+ years and during discontinuation; safety signals over time may become more salient as absolute exposure accumulates.*
