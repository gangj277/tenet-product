# Entropy Collapse: Empirical Detection and Recovery Limits in AI Systems

**Publication Info:** Preprints.org (Submitted: 11 January 2026, Posted: 12 January 2026)
**Author:** Michael Aaron Cody

## Summary

This paper introduces a novel, fully empirical system for detecting "entropy collapse" in feedback-driven AI systems. Entropy collapse is defined not merely by low entropy, but by the failure of interventions to restore diversity within a finite observation window. The framework relies on observable quantities like state distributions, Shannon entropy, dominance concentration, and distributional changes. By conducting repeated intervention experiments, the system establishes a recovery-based threshold, indicating when collapse becomes statistically irreversible. This approach offers a practical tool for early warning, evaluation, and intervention in AI and socio-technical systems, grounding collapse in measurable irrecoverability rather than descriptive convergence.

## Key Claims

*   Contemporary AI systems increasingly rely on recursive feedback processes (self-training, preference optimization, algorithmic governance).
*   A recurring failure mode in these systems is behavioral contraction, dominance emergence, and loss of intervention effectiveness, often termed "entropy collapse."
*   Existing work largely describes collapse but lacks a general empirical criterion for declaring irrecoverable states.
*   This paper proposes an operational definition of collapse based on the failure of admissible interventions to restore diversity.
*   The framework uses exclusively observable quantities: empirical state distributions, Shannon entropy, dominance concentration, distributional displacement, and second-order change in displacement.
*   A recovery-based threshold is constructed from repeated intervention experiments to identify statistically irreversible collapse.
*   The empirical indicators can generalize to socio-technical systems, providing a common language for diagnosing rigidity and adaptability loss.
