# AI Model Collapse: Causes and Prevention

**Publication:** WitnessAI Blog
**Date:** November 24, 2025

## Summary

This article from WitnessAI addresses the emerging risk of "AI model collapse," a phenomenon where large language models (LLMs) degrade over time due to recursive training on AI-generated data. This process leads to a loss of data diversity, accuracy, and meaning, resulting in incoherent, biased, or nonsensical outputs. The article explains the causes, consequences, and signs of model collapse, emphasizing that it is not inevitable but requires proactive mitigation strategies. Key prevention methods include prioritizing human-generated data, tracking data provenance, accumulating new original data, using high-quality synthetic data judiciously, and implementing AI governance tools.

## Key Claims

*   AI model collapse is the progressive degradation of an AI model's performance caused by training on data generated by other AI systems.
*   This recursive process leads to a loss of data diversity, accuracy, and meaning, causing outputs to become less coherent and eventually collapse.
*   Consequences include loss of output quality, bias amplification, erosion of real-world alignment, degradation of future model generations, and economic/security impacts.
*   Causes include recursive data training, reduced access to original data, synthetic data contamination, feedback loops in data aggregation, and lack of provenance tracking.
*   Signs of collapse include decreased output diversity, higher factual inaccuracy, unnatural phrasing, loss of novelty, and reduced correlation to training objectives.
*   Model collapse is not inevitable and can be prevented through proper data curation, oversight, and governance.
*   Prevention strategies involve prioritizing human-generated data, tracking data history, leveraging data accumulation, using high-quality synthetic data, and implementing AI governance tools.

## Relevant Evidence/Quotes

*   "AI model collapse refers to the progressive degradation of an AI model’s performance caused by training on data that has been generated by other AI systems rather than original, human-generated content."
*   "When a new model is trained primarily or partially on AI-generated data — such as text or images produced by previous LLMs — it inherits the biases, errors, and distortions embedded in those outputs."
*   "Over multiple training cycles, recursively generated data causes statistical distortions known as data distribution drift."
*   "The concept of model collapse was formally explored in the 2023 paper ‘The Curse of Recursion: Training on Generated Data Makes Models Forget’ by Ilia Shumailov, Zakhar Shumaylov, and Yiren Zhao."
*   "Preventing model collapse requires a multi-layered data management and governance strategy that prioritizes diversity, transparency, and ongoing validation."
*   "Prioritize human-generated data wherever possible. Curate datasets from verified, real-world sources such as academic publications, government archives, and peer-reviewed content."
