# Track: San Diego Poster Session 3 - NeurIPS

**Publication:** NeurIPS 2025

## Summary

This document is a collection of abstracts from Poster Session 3 at NeurIPS 2025. The presented research covers a diverse range of topics within machine learning and artificial intelligence. Key areas explored include methods for improving model interpretability (Channel Matters), advancements in optimization algorithms for nonconvex problems (A Regularized Newton Method), novel approaches to Neural Architecture Search (Per-Architecture Training-Free Metric Optimization), and techniques for efficient knowledge distillation (SDPGO). Other topics include learning heuristics for job shop scheduling (MIStar), zeroth-order optimization for training deep neural networks (PseuZO), network simplification via pruning (ReplaceMe), fault-tolerant optimization for LLM training (MeCeFO), ethical considerations of real-time generative AI (Digital Heroin), a broader conception of rigor in AI research (Rigor in AI), the unification of physical and social dynamics in world models, scalable data valuation (KAIROS), knowledge distillation detection, strategic classification incentives, watermark removal in AI-generated images, statistical frameworks for LLM watermarking, the need for a model atlas, and technically-informed regulation for military AI.

## Key Claims

*   **Channel Matters:** A novel Channel-wise Influence (ChInf) method can effectively estimate the influence of different channels in Multivariate Time Series (MTS) analysis, leading to improved performance in tasks like anomaly detection and data pruning.
*   **A Regularized Newton Method:** A new class of regularized Newton methods can achieve optimal global complexity and quadratic local convergence for nonconvex optimization without prior knowledge of the Hessian Lipschitz constant.
*   **Per-Architecture Training-Free Metric Optimization:** Dynamically optimizing combinations of multiple training-free metrics for each architecture, rather than globally, can enhance Neural Architecture Search (NAS) efficiency and final architecture performance.
*   **SDPGO:** A self-knowledge distillation framework (SDPGO) that uses gradient information to weight features significantly impacting classification performance can lead to improved learning and surpass state-of-the-art knowledge distillation techniques.
*   **MIStar:** A Memory-enhanced Improvement Search framework with heterogeneous graph representation (MIStar) can significantly outperform traditional heuristics and state-of-the-art DRL-based constructive methods for flexible job-shop scheduling.
*   **PseuZO:** A Pseudo-Zeroth-Order (PseuZO) framework can optimize composite objective functions, especially large-scale models, by estimating the Jacobian matrix and using exponential moving average to reduce variance, achieving better convergence and memory reduction compared to existing methods.
*   **ReplaceMe:** A generalized training-free depth pruning method (ReplaceMe) can effectively replace transformer blocks with linear operations, achieving significant pruning ratios while retaining high performance without additional training.
*   **MeCeFO:** A Memory- and Computation-efficient Fault-tolerant Optimization (MeCeFO) algorithm can ensure robust LLM training with minimal overhead during node failures by employing skip-connection, recomputation, and low-rank gradient approximation.
*   **Digital Heroin:** Real-time, hyper-personalized generative AI poses a significant risk of becoming the next wave of addictive digital media, necessitating strong government oversight and proactive ethical guidelines from the ML community.
*   **Rigor in AI:** A broader conception of rigor in AI research, encompassing methodological, epistemic, normative, conceptual, reporting, and interpretative aspects, is needed to address responsible AI concerns and overblown capability claims.
*   **World Models:** The next crucial frontier for world models is the systematic, bidirectional unification of physical and social predictive capabilities to holistically integrate objective physical laws with subjective social dynamics.
*   **KAIROS:** A model-agnostic framework (KAIROS) that values examples by their contribution to the Maximum Mean Discrepancy (MMD) between the training set and a clean reference distribution offers scalable and efficient data valuation, outperforming existing methods.
*   **Knowledge Distillation Detection:** A model-agnostic framework combining data-free input synthesis and statistical score computation can effectively detect knowledge distillation in open-weights models, improving detection accuracy over baselines.
*   **Incentivizing Desirable Effort Profiles:** Understanding the causal structure of features and uncertainty is crucial for designing strategic classification systems that incentivize agents to invest effort in desirable features, especially under incomplete information.
*   **The Future Unmarked:** A semantic-level removal attack (NFPA) that formulates watermark removal as a video generation task can effectively remove watermarks from AI-generated images, highlighting vulnerabilities in current watermarking methods.
*   **Statistical Framework of Watermarks for LLMs:** A general and flexible framework for reasoning about the statistical efficiency of LLM watermarks and designing powerful detection rules can lead to optimal detection rules with competitive or superior power compared to existing approaches.
*   **Model Atlas:** Charting the world's model population in a unified structure (Model Atlas) is necessary to address issues of undocumented models, wasted resources, propagated biases, and missed opportunities, requiring new ML methods to infer properties directly from model weights.
*   **Military AI Regulation:** Regulation for military AI, particularly AI-powered lethal autonomous weapon systems (AI-LAWS), must be technically-informed and behavior-based, involving AI researchers throughout the lifecycle to mitigate risks like escalation and erosion of human oversight.

## Relevant Evidence/Quotes

*   **Channel Matters:** "Particularly, no previous work studied the effects of channel information of MTS in order to explore counterfactual effects between these channels and model performance. To fill this gap, we propose a novel Channel-wise Influence (ChInf) method that is the first to estimate the influence of different channels in MTS."
*   **A Regularized Newton Method:** "Whether a parameter-free algorithm of this type can simultaneously achieve optimal global complexity and quadratic local convergence remains an open question. To bridge this long-standing gap, we propose a new class of regularizers constructed from the current and previous gradients..."
*   **SDPGO:** "Specifically, the proposed framework refines the gradient information into a dynamically changing weighting factor to evaluate the distillation knowledge via the dynamic weight adjustment scheme."
*   **PseuZO:** "Our algorithm achieves an $O( \\max \\lbrace \\alpha_1 L\\epsilon^{-2}, \\alpha_1 L \\sigma_2^2\\epsilon^{-4} \\rbrace )$ convergence rate, where $\\alpha_1$ is the effective dimension of $\\mathcal{F}$." and "For instance, under the same computation time, with respect to SST2 task, PesuZO gets 9.8\\% higher accuracy than MeZO (91.2\\% v.s. 82.4\\%)."
*   **ReplaceMe:** "Applied to several large language models (LLMs), ReplaceMe achieves up to 25% pruning while retaining approximately 90% of the original model’s performance on open benchmarks—without any training or healing steps, resulting in minimal computational overhead."
*   **MeCeFO:** "Theoretically, MeCeFO matches the convergence rate of conventional distributed training, with a rate of $\\mathcal{O}(1/\\sqrt{nT})$, where $n$ is the data parallelism size and $T$ is the number of iterations. Empirically, MeCeFO maintains robust performance under high failure rates, incurring only a 4.18\\% drop in throughput, demonstrating $5.0\\times$ to $6.7\\times$ greater resilience than previous SOTA approaches."
*   **Digital Heroin:** "By shortening the content-generation feedback loop to mere seconds, these advanced models will soon be able to hyper-personalize outputs on the fly. When paired with misaligned incentives (e.g., maximizing user engagement), this will fuel unprecedented compulsive consumption patterns with far-reaching consequences for mental health, cognitive development, and social stability."
*   **Rigor in AI:** "We argue that this narrow conception of rigor has contributed to the concerns raised by the responsible AI community, including overblown claims about the capabilities of AI systems. Our position is that a broader conception of what rigorous AI research and practice should entail is needed."
*   **World Models:** "This position paper argues that the systematic, bidirectional unification of physical and social predictive capabilities is the next crucial frontier for world model development."
*   **KAIROS:** "Unlike Wasserstein methods, MMD admits a closed-form solution that requires no approximations and is scalable to large datasets." and "On ImageNet, KAIROS achieves up to 15 $\\times$ speedup over the fastest baseline while maintaining superior data valuation quality."
*   **Knowledge Distillation Detection:** "Experiments on diverse architectures for image classification and text-to-image generation show that our method improves detection accuracy over the strongest baselines by 59.6\\% on CIFAR-10, 71.2\\% on ImageNet, and 20.0\\% for text-to-image generation."
*   **The Future Unmarked:** "In this paper, we propose Next Frame Prediction Attack (NFPA), the first semantic-level removal attack. Unlike pixel-level attacks, NFPA formulates watermark removal as a video generation task: it treats the watermarked image as the initial frame and aims to subtly manipulate the image semantics to generate the next-frame image, i.e., the unwatermarked image."
*   **Statistical Framework of Watermarks for LLMs:** "In this paper, we introduce a general and flexible framework for reasoning about the statistical efficiency of watermarks and designing powerful detection rules."
*   **Model Atlas:** "Public model repositories now contain millions of models, yet most remain undocumented and effectively lost: their capabilities, provenance, and constraints cannot be reliably determined."
*   **Military AI Regulation:** "We argue that AI researchers must be involved throughout the regulatory lifecycle. Thus, we propose a clear, behavior-based definition of AI-LAWS—systems that introduce unique risks through their use of modern AI—as a foundation for technically grounded regulation, given that existing frameworks do not distinguish them from conventional LAWS."

## Contribution to Research Frame

This collection of abstracts contributes to the research frame by showcasing a wide array of cutting-edge AI research, many of which touch upon themes relevant to the core concerns of model collapse, data quality, and responsible AI development.

*   **Model Collapse & Data Quality:** Several papers implicitly or explicitly address issues related to data quality and the integrity of learned distributions. For instance, **KAIROS** (Poster #101) directly tackles data valuation, suggesting methods to identify valuable training examples, which is crucial for preventing the degradation of data distributions that underlies model collapse. **SDPGO** (Poster #1002) and **ReplaceMe** (Poster #1005) focus on improving model training and efficiency, which could indirectly impact how models handle synthetic data or compression, potentially influencing collapse dynamics. **PseuZO** (Poster #1004) and **MeCeFO** (Poster #1006) offer advancements in optimization and fault tolerance for large models, which are relevant to the stability and reliability of training processes that might be susceptible to collapse. **The Future Unmarked** (Poster #1012) and **Statistical Framework of Watermarks for LLMs** (Poster #1013) highlight the challenges of provenance and authenticity in generated content, a domain where model collapse could manifest as degraded output quality.
*   **Responsible AI & Ethical Considerations:** The research frame's emphasis on responsible AI is directly addressed by several papers. **Digital Heroin** (Poster #1007) and **Military AI Needs Technically-Informed Regulation** (Poster #1015) are explicit calls for ethical considerations and regulation, highlighting the societal impact of AI. **Rigor in AI** (Poster #1008) argues for a broader understanding of rigor, which is essential for robustly evaluating AI systems and their potential risks, including those associated with model collapse. **World Models Should Prioritize the Unification of Physical and Social Dynamics** (Poster #1009) points to the need for more comprehensive world models, which could be more resilient to collapse if they better capture complex real-world interactions.
*   **Technical Advancements:** Papers like **A Regularized Newton Method for Nonconvex Optimization** (Poster #1000) and **SDPGO** (Poster #1002) present algorithmic innovations that could be instrumental in developing more stable and efficient training procedures, potentially offering solutions to mitigate model collapse. **Channel Matters** (Poster #100) and **Per-Architecture Training-Free Metric Optimization** (Poster #1001) explore novel ways to understand and optimize model performance, which could lead to insights into how data characteristics influence model behavior and susceptibility to collapse.

Overall, this session provides a snapshot of current research directions that are either directly tackling problems related to data integrity and model stability or are developing foundational techniques and ethical frameworks that are critical for addressing the challenges posed by model collapse in generative AI.
