# NeurIPS 2025 Posters: Per-Source Summary

**Source:** NeurIPS 2025 Posters

**Publication Info:** NeurIPS 2025 Conference Proceedings

## Summary

This collection of posters from NeurIPS 2025 showcases a diverse range of advancements in machine learning. Key themes include novel approaches to video anomaly detection using LLMs, enhancing sample selection in noisy label learning, and developing new datasets for Parkinson's disease gait assessment. Other contributions explore adaptive clipping for differential privacy, counterfactual reasoning for language model agents, theoretical limitations and improvements for Transformers, and learning-based methods for linear integer arithmetic interpolation. Additionally, research is presented on refined scaling laws for LLMs, single-positive multi-label learning, guidance techniques for diffusion models, feature visualization manipulation, synthetic data augmentation for recognition tasks, non-convex optimization, integrating graph structures with LLMs for combinatorial optimization, online allocation problems, and depth estimation from event-based data.

## Key Claims

*   **MoniTor:** A novel memory-based online scoring queue scheme (MoniTor) effectively addresses online video anomaly detection by leveraging VLMs and incorporating LSTM-inspired prediction mechanisms and a scoring queue with anomaly priors.
*   **Early Cutting:** Mislabeled Easy Examples (MEEs), which are mislabeled and correctly predicted early in training, are particularly harmful; the proposed "Early Cutting" method uses later training states to re-select confident subsets, effectively filtering out MEEs.
*   **Care-PD:** The Care-PD dataset is introduced as the largest publicly available archive of 3D mesh gait data for Parkinson's Disease, supporting benchmarks for clinical score prediction and unsupervised motion tasks, and demonstrating the value of curated, diverse training data.
*   **Adaptive Sigmoid Clipping (AdaSig):** AdaSig is a novel clipping method for differentially private learning that uses a sigmoid function to adaptively balance direction and magnitude mismatch, retaining optimal convergence rates.
*   **Abstract Counterfactuals:** This framework enables counterfactual reasoning for language model agents by focusing on high-level action characteristics, producing more consistent and meaningful counterfactuals than token-level methods.
*   **Strassen Attention:** This mechanism theoretically overcomes limitations of one-layer softmax transformers on tasks requiring advanced reasoning and compositionality, offering sub-cubic running time complexity.
*   **Constant Bit-size Transformers:** It is proven that constant bit-size transformers with sufficiently long context windows are Turing complete, and the complexity class SPACE$[s(n)]$ characterizes their expressive power.
*   **Learning Simple Interpolants for LIA:** A lightweight, learning-based approach is proposed to generate simpler interpolants for linear integer arithmetic, reducing the complexity of interpolants produced by solvers like Z3.
*   **Farseer:** This refined scaling law for LLMs offers enhanced predictive accuracy across scales, outperforming existing laws like Chinchilla's and enabling reliable evaluation of training strategies.
*   **Crisp:** This framework for single-positive multi-label learning estimates class priors and uses them to derive an unbiased risk estimator, improving performance over methods assuming identical class priors.
*   **Token Perturbation Guidance (TPG):** TPG is a novel, training-free, and condition-agnostic guidance method for diffusion models that applies perturbation matrices to token representations, enhancing generation quality and prompt alignment.
*   **Gradient Slingshots:** This method allows for manipulation of feature visualizations in DNNs without altering the model architecture or performance, exposing vulnerabilities in relying solely on feature visualizations.
*   **AugGen:** This self-contained synthetic augmentation technique uses a class-conditional generative model trained solely on the target dataset to improve recognition performance, particularly in data-limited scenarios.
*   **Best Response Flow for Non-convex Optimization:** The convergence of Best Response flow for minimizing non-convex functionals is studied, demonstrating how to choose regularizers to ensure convergence to a unique global minimizer.
*   **STRCMP:** This structure-aware LLM framework integrates graph neural networks with LLMs to discover algorithms for combinatorial optimization, outperforming existing methods by leveraging structural priors.
*   **Learning-Augmented Online Allocation:** A general framework for online allocation problems is developed, producing nearly optimal solutions using a single learned weight vector, with applications in routing, scheduling, and fair allocation.
*   **DERD-Net:** This framework for event-based depth estimation uses disparity space images and a hybrid convolutional-recurrent network to achieve state-of-the-art performance in monocular and stereo setups.
*   **Tight Lower Bounds and Improved Convergence in Performative Prediction:** New algorithms (Affine Risk Minimizers) are introduced that converge to performatively stable points for a broader class of problems, and new tight bounds are proven.
*   **BNMusic:** This framework blends environmental noises into personalized music generated from text prompts, offering an alternative to acoustic masking for reducing noise annoyance.

## Contribution to Research Frame

This collection of posters contributes to the research frame by providing empirical evidence and theoretical advancements related to the sustainability and robustness of machine learning models, particularly in the context of generative AI and large-scale training.

*   **Model Collapse (Degradation):** Several papers implicitly or explicitly touch upon issues that could lead to or mitigate model collapse.
    *   **MoniTor** and **DERD-Net** focus on improving performance in specific domains (video anomaly detection, depth estimation) by developing more robust models, which could indirectly contribute to understanding how to maintain performance over time or with complex data.
    *   **Farseer** directly addresses the scaling gap and predictive accuracy for LLMs, suggesting that better scaling laws can lead to more reliable insights from smaller-scale experiments, potentially helping to avoid pitfalls in large-scale training that might resemble collapse.
    *   **AugGen** and **STRCMP** explore synthetic data generation and structure-aware methods, respectively. While not directly about collapse, the success of these methods in improving performance suggests that well-controlled synthetic data or structure integration can be beneficial, contrasting with uncontrolled recursive generation.
    *   **Gradient Slingshots** and **TPG** highlight vulnerabilities and new guidance mechanisms in generative models (DNNs, diffusion models). Understanding how to manipulate or guide these models is crucial for controlling their output and potentially preventing undesirable degradation.
    *   **Constant Bit-size Transformers** and **Strassen Attention** delve into the theoretical capabilities and limitations of Transformer architectures. Understanding these fundamental limits is key to designing models that can generalize and maintain performance, rather than collapsing.
*   **Distribution Drift and Tail-end Diversity:**
    *   **Abstract Counterfactuals** and **Performative Prediction** directly address how predictions and actions can influence future data distributions. This is highly relevant to understanding distribution drift, as the "predictions" of a generative model can become the "data" for its next iteration. The work on performative prediction, in particular, aims to find stable points, which is the antithesis of collapse.
    *   The focus on **sample selection** (Early Cutting) and **differential privacy** (Adaptive Sigmoid Clipping) in other papers speaks to methods that aim to maintain data integrity and prevent certain types of noise or bias from dominating, which are related to preserving the diversity and quality of the training distribution.
    *   **Care-PD** highlights the importance of diverse, clinically annotated datasets. The creation and use of such datasets are crucial for ensuring that models trained on them do not suffer from a lack of representation, a potential precursor to collapse if synthetic data were to oversimplify or erase these nuances.
*   **Mitigation Strategies:**
    *   While not explicitly framed as "mitigation strategies for model collapse," many papers propose novel techniques that could serve this purpose. For instance, **Farseer's** refined scaling laws could guide resource allocation to prevent training regimes that might lead to collapse.
    *   The development of **Abstract Counterfactuals** offers a way to reason about LM agent behavior, which could be used to audit or refine synthetic data generation processes.
    *   **AugGen** demonstrates successful synthetic data augmentation, suggesting that carefully controlled synthetic data generation can be beneficial, rather than detrimental, when integrated properly.

In essence, these posters contribute by exploring the fundamental capabilities of models, proposing methods to improve data handling and generation, and developing frameworks to understand and control model behavior in complex scenarios, all of which are pertinent to understanding the boundaries and potential remedies for model collapse.
