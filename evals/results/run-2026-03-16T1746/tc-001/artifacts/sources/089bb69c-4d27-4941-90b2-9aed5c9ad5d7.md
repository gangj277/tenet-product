# Knowledge Collapse in LLMs: When Fluency Survives but Facts Fail under Recursive Synthetic Training

**Authors:** Figarri Keisha, Zekun Wu, Ze Wang, Adriano Koshiyama, Philip Treleaven
**Affiliation:** Holistic AI, University College London

## Summary

This paper introduces and defines "knowledge collapse" as a three-stage phenomenon occurring in Large Language Models (LLMs) trained recursively on synthetic data. This collapse is characterized by a decline in factual accuracy while maintaining surface-level fluency, leading to "confidently wrong" outputs. The research demonstrates that the trajectory of this collapse is dependent on instruction format, distinguishing it from traditional model collapse. A proposed mitigation strategy, domain-specific synthetic training, significantly improves resistance to this collapse, delaying accuracy decay by 15 times compared to general synthetic training. The authors also provide an evaluation framework for assessing this epistemic deterioration.

## Key Claims

*   Recursive training on synthetic data leads to "knowledge collapse," a process where factual accuracy degrades while fluency persists.
*   Knowledge collapse is a three-stage phenomenon: Stage A (Knowledge Preservation), Stage B (Knowledge Collapse - the "confidently wrong" transition), and Stage C (Instruction-following Collapse).
*   The timing and trajectory of knowledge collapse are conditional on the instruction format used, unlike traditional prompt-agnostic model collapse.
*   Domain-specific synthetic training is an effective mitigation strategy, showing a 15x improvement in collapse resistance and delaying accuracy decay.
*   Existing evaluation metrics may fail to detect Stage B collapse, where models appear competent but are factually incorrect.

## Relevant Evidence/Quotes

*   "We define knowledge collapse as a distinct three-stage phenomenon where factual accuracy deteriorates while surface fluency persists, creating 'confidently wrong' outputs that pose critical risks in accuracy-dependent domains."
*   "Through controlled experiments with recursive synthetic training, we demonstrate that collapse trajectory and timing depend critically on instruction format, distinguishing instruction-following collapse from traditional model collapse through its conditional, prompt-dependent nature."
*   "We propose domain-specific synthetic training as a targeted mitigation strategy that achieves substantial improvements in collapse resistance while maintaining computational efficiency."
*   "Stage B (Knowledge Collapse) demonstrates the critical transition where factual accuracy deteriorates while task format adherence persists, the 'confidently wrong' phenomenon where models produce well-formatted but factually incorrect responses."

## Contribution to Research Frame

This source directly addresses the core concerns of the research frame by investigating the technical validity and inevitability of model collapse. It provides a nuanced definition of "knowledge collapse" that goes beyond general distributional drift, focusing on the critical divergence between fluency and factual accuracy. The paper empirically demonstrates that collapse is not an absolute certainty but is influenced by specific factors like instruction format, suggesting it is a manageable engineering challenge rather than a fundamental mathematical law. Furthermore, it proposes and validates a concrete mitigation strategy (domain-specific training), directly contributing to the understanding of interventions that can prevent the loss of tail-end distribution diversity and maintain model stability. The findings support the hypothesis that strategic data curation and training hygiene can indeed delay or prevent collapse.
