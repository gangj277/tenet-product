# Project Overview: Model Collapse From Recursive Synthetic Data Training

## 1. Original Research Question
Under what conditions does recursive training on synthetic data lead to model collapse in generative models?

## 2. Interpreted Research Objective
To determine the technical thresholds and data composition strategies that differentiate catastrophic model collapse from sustainable recursive training, specifically identifying whether collapse is an inherent property of synthetic data loops or a consequence of specific training workflows.

## 3. Inferred Research Frame
The working hypothesis is that model collapse is a manageable risk rather than a fundamental law of generative AI. The research frame posits that collapse is contingent upon the ratio of "fresh" real-world data to synthetic data and the specific sampling and accumulation methods employed during training.

## 4. Scope Boundaries
*   **In-Scope:** Machine learning literature regarding recursive training, synthetic data reuse, distribution degradation, and mathematical proofs of convergence/divergence in generative loops.
*   **Out-of-Scope:** Product-level commentary, non-technical blog posts, and general discussions on AI ethics or copyright that do not address the technical mechanics of model training.
*   **Time Horizon:** 2023–2025.
*   **Geography:** Global literature.
