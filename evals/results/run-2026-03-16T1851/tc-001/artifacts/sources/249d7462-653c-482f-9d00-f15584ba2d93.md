# A Guide to AI Model Collapse

**Publication Info:** Jake Handy, October 23, 2024 (Paid Subscription on Substack)

## Summary

This article introduces "model collapse," a significant threat to generative AI where models degrade over successive training generations, particularly when trained on their own synthetic outputs. The author frames this phenomenon as a technical nightmare, exploring its underlying causes and potential solutions. While acknowledging the severity of the risk, the piece hints at the possibility of preventing or mitigating collapse through innovative approaches.

## Key Claims

*   Model collapse is a degradation phenomenon occurring in AI systems.
*   It is considered a prominent existential threat to AI.
*   The core issue involves AI models being trained on their own synthetic outputs.
*   The article aims to explain what model collapse means and explore ways to prevent it.
