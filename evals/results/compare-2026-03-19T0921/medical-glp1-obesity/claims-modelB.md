## Claim 1 (Strongest evidence): Semaglutide 2.4 mg and tirzepatide produce clinically superior weight loss vs placebo in adults with obesity/overweight without diabetes

- **Claim statement:** Semaglutide 2.4 mg and tirzepatide (all studied doses) achieve markedly greater weight reduction than placebo in non-diabetic adults over ~68–72 weeks.
- **Supporting sources:**
  - Semaglutide 2.4 mg reduced body weight by **-14.9% vs -2.4% with placebo** after **68 weeks** in non-diabetic obesity/overweight.
    - [Source: 500c72dd-825a-4964-9627-d776aaf05dad]
  - Tirzepatide reduced body weight by **15.0% (5 mg), 19.5% (10 mg), and 20.9% (15 mg)** vs **3.1% with placebo** after **72 weeks** in people with obesity but **without diabetes**.
    - [Source: a497e563-aa2f-4ee1-9430-db018301bcbb]
- **Contradicting sources:** None explicitly contradict the placebo-controlled superiority in non-diabetic populations.
- **Confidence assessment:** **High** — large, consistent relative effects reported in non-diabetic cohorts and summarized across higher-level sources.

## Claim 3: Gastrointestinal adverse events (especially nausea and diarrhea) are the primary tolerability limitation and show dose-related effects

- **Claim statement:** Nausea and diarrhea occur significantly more often with GLP-1-based therapies (e.g., semaglutide) than placebo, are **dose-dependent**, and contribute to discontinuation risk and reduced tolerability.
- **Supporting sources:**
  - Semaglutide: nausea (**RR 2.58**) and diarrhea (**RR 2.01**) occurred significantly more frequently than control; subgroup analysis suggests dose-dependent effects.
    - [Source: 567d18dd-0fc4-4773-a3bf-d531739526a0]
  - Mechanistic/clinical synthesis: dose-dependent GI effects limit the therapeutic index of GLP-1 receptor agonists.
    - [Source: da8c7eae-b56f-42dc-be6e-ebbeed9d5c87]
- **Contradicting sources:** None reported.
- **Confidence assessment:** **High** — effect direction and GI focus are consistent across the provided reviews.
