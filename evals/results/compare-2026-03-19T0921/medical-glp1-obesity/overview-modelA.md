# Research Overview: GLP-1 Therapies for Obesity

## 1. Research Question
What does recent literature say about the efficacy and safety of GLP-1-based therapies for obesity management in adults without diabetes?

## 2. Research Objective
To produce a clinically grounded synthesis of weight-loss efficacy, cardiometabolic benefits, and safety tradeoffs, with a specific focus on the comparative profiles of semaglutide and tirzepatide in non-diabetic populations.

## 3. Research Frame
This project operates under the **"Efficacy-Tolerability Tradeoff"** hypothesis. The working premise is that while GLP-1 receptor agonists and dual agonists provide transformative, dose-dependent weight loss and metabolic protection, their real-world clinical utility is constrained by gastrointestinal intolerance, high discontinuation rates, and the requirement for chronic administration to prevent weight regain.

## 4. Scope Boundaries
*   **Population:** Adults with obesity or overweight, specifically excluding those with type 2 diabetes where possible.
*   **Interventions:** Semaglutide (2.4mg) and Tirzepatide.
*   **Outcomes:** Weight-loss magnitude, cardiometabolic markers (BP, lipids, CRP), and safety/adverse event profiles.
*   **Time Horizon:** 2022–2025.
*   **Evidence Type:** Systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and pivotal Phase III clinical trials.
