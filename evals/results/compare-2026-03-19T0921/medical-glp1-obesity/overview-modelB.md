# Overview: GLP-1 Therapies for Obesity (Adults Without Diabetes)

## 1. Original research question
**What does recent literature say about the efficacy and safety of GLP-1-based therapies for obesity management in adults without diabetes?**

## 2. Interpreted research objective
To synthesize, using high-level evidence (primarily systematic reviews/meta-analyses and supporting trials where available), the **clinical balance of benefit vs harm** of GLP-1 receptor agonists and related incretin-based therapies for **adult obesity in non-diabetic populations**, with particular emphasis on:
- **Weight-loss efficacy** (magnitude and responder thresholds)
- **Cardiometabolic effects** (e.g., blood pressure, inflammatory markers, lipid changes)
- **Safety and tolerability** (adverse event profile, discontinuation pressures, and clinically relevant risk signals)
- **Comparative interpretation** between **semaglutide** and **tirzepatide** where the evidence permits.

## 3. Inferred research frame / working hypothesis under examination
**“Efficacy–Tolerability Tradeoff” frame**

GLP-1-based therapies are expected to produce **dose-dependent, clinically meaningful weight loss** and **cardiometabolic improvements**, but their real-world clinical utility is constrained by:
- **Gastrointestinal (GI) intolerance** (a major driver of discontinuation)
- **Heterogeneity across studies** (baseline risk, lifestyle/background interventions, trial design and duration)
- **Chronic-treatment dependence** (weight regain after discontinuation)
- **Uncertainties in longer-term safety** (especially beyond the typical trial windows)

Working hypothesis being examined:  
**GLP-1-based therapies appear to produce clinically meaningful weight loss and cardiometabolic improvement, but adverse effects and study heterogeneity materially affect interpretation.**

## 4. Scope boundaries
- **Population:** Adults with **obesity** (or overweight with obesity risk) **without diabetes**, prioritized where possible.
- **Interventions:** GLP-1-based therapies relevant to obesity management, with attention to:
  - **Semaglutide** (e.g., 2.4 mg regimen as the key obesity-labeled dose)
  - **Tirzepatide** (multiple doses)
  - GLP-1 receptor agonists broadly, as needed for safety context.
- **Outcomes:**
  - Primary: **Weight loss magnitude** and **proportions achieving clinically meaningful thresholds** (e.g., >5%, >10%, >15–20%).
  - Secondary: **Cardiometabolic endpoints** reported in the evidence base (blood pressure, lipids, inflammatory markers).
  - Safety: **GI adverse events**, discontinuation, and other clinically notable risk signals (e.g., biliary disease, pancreatitis reports).
- **Evidence type and timing:** Emphasis on **recent literature** (project time horizon: **2022–2025**), prioritizing **systematic reviews and meta-analyses**.
- **Geography:** Global evidence; no country restrictions.
- **Exclusions/limitations (implicit):**
  - Where non-diabetic evidence is thin, diabetes-inclusive trials may appear in the literature landscape but are treated cautiously for inference to the non-diabetic population.
  - Long-term outcomes beyond trial durations are treated as **open evidence gaps** rather than assumed.
