## Toward Conditional Distribution Calibration in Survival Prediction

**Publication:** 38th Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS 2024)

**Authors:** Shi-ang Qi, Yakun Yu, Russell Greiner

This paper introduces the CiPOT (Conformalized survival distribution using Individual survival Probability at Observed Time) framework, a novel post-processing method designed to improve the calibration of survival prediction models, particularly addressing the critical need for *conditional calibration*. Traditional methods often focus on marginal calibration or discrimination, sometimes at the expense of the other. CiPOT leverages conformal prediction with a specific conformity score (iPOT) that accounts for censored data and the inherent variability of individual survival distributions. The authors provide theoretical guarantees for both marginal and conditional calibration and demonstrate through extensive experiments on 15 diverse datasets that CiPOT effectively enhances calibration without compromising discrimination.

### Key Claims:

*   Conditional distribution calibration is crucial for real-world applications, especially for individual decision-making, and is often inadequate with marginal calibration alone.
*   The proposed CiPOT framework, based on conformal prediction and the iPOT score, effectively improves both marginal and conditional calibration in survival prediction.
*   CiPOT can handle censored data by adapting the conformity score to reflect the updated possible range of survival probabilities.
*   The method theoretically guarantees asymptotic marginal and conditional calibration under specified assumptions.
*   Experiments across 15 datasets show that CiPOT improves calibration without sacrificing discriminative ability and is computationally efficient.
*   CiPOT preserves the monotonicity of survival distributions, unlike some other conformal methods like CSD.

### Contribution to Research Frame:

This source directly addresses the research frame's focus on understanding and improving model calibration. Specifically, it introduces a novel method (CiPOT) that explicitly tackles *conditional calibration* in survival prediction, a more nuanced form of calibration than typically considered. The paper's emphasis on the practical implications of conditional calibration for individual decision-making aligns with the research frame's interest in actionable insights. The theoretical guarantees and extensive empirical validation provide strong evidence for the effectiveness of their approach, contributing a concrete methodology for achieving better calibrated survival models. The comparison with existing methods like CSD also highlights the advantages of CiPOT, particularly in handling censorship and maintaining monotonicity, which are crucial aspects for robust model performance.
