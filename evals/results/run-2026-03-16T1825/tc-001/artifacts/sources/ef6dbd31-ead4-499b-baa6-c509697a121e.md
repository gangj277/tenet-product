# Is Model Collapse Inevitable? Breaking the Curse of Recursion by Accumulating Real and Synthetic Data

**Authors:** Matthias Gerstgrasser, Rylan Schaeffer, Apratim Dey, Rafael Rafailov, Dhruv Pai, Henry Sleight, John Hughes, Tomasz Korbak, Rajashree Agrawal, Andrey Gromov, Daniel A. Roberts, Diyi Yang, David Donoho, Sanmi Koyejo

**Publication Info:** Stanford University, University of Maryland, College Park, MIT & Sequoia Capital

## Summary

This paper investigates the phenomenon of "model collapse," where generative models trained on their own outputs progressively degrade in performance. Previous studies often assumed that new synthetic data replaces old data, leading to collapse. However, this research explores a more realistic scenario where data accumulates over time. Through empirical studies on language models, diffusion models for molecule generation, and VAEs for image generation, the authors demonstrate that accumulating synthetic data alongside original real data effectively avoids model collapse. This finding is further supported by a theoretical framework using linear models, which proves that accumulating data leads to a finite upper bound on test error, thus preventing collapse, unlike the data replacement strategy which results in unbounded error.

## Key Claims

*   Training generative models on their own outputs can lead to "model collapse," a progressive degradation of performance.
*   Previous studies on model collapse largely assumed data replacement, where new synthetic data replaces old data.
*   A more realistic scenario involves data accumulation, where new synthetic data is added to the existing dataset.
*   Empirical experiments across language models, diffusion models, and VAEs show that accumulating data with real data avoids model collapse.
*   Replacing data with synthetic data consistently leads to model collapse in the tested models and modalities.
*   A theoretical framework using linear models confirms that accumulating data results in a finite, bounded test error, preventing collapse.
*   Data replacement, in contrast, leads to a test error that increases linearly with the number of model-fitting iterations.

## Contribution to or Challenge of the Research Frame

This source directly supports the working hypothesis that "the strategic integration of real-world data and specific architectural interventions can mitigate these effects" of model collapse. Specifically, it provides strong empirical and theoretical evidence that **accumulating real data alongside synthetic data is a key intervention that avoids model collapse**. This challenges the notion that model collapse is an inevitable consequence of recursive training, aligning with the inferred research frame that "model collapse is not an inherent law of recursive training but a manageable risk dependent on data hygiene, the ratio of real-to-synthetic data...". The paper directly addresses the "ratio of real-to-synthetic data" by showing that *keeping* the real data and *adding* synthetic data prevents collapse, implying that a sufficient proportion of real data is crucial. It also provides evidence against the idea that "models trained on 100% synthetic data show immediate performance decay" by demonstrating that *mixing* with real data prevents this decay. The theoretical proof further solidifies the idea that the *dynamics* of data usage (replacement vs. accumulation) are critical, not just the presence of synthetic data itself.
