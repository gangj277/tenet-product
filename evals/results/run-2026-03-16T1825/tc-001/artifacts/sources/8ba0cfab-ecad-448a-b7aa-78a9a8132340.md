# COLLAPSE OR THRIVE? PERILS AND PROMISES OF SYNTHETIC DATA IN A SELF-GENERATING WORLD

**Publication:** Paper under double-blind review

## Summary

This paper investigates the phenomenon of "model collapse," where generative AI models trained on data produced by previous models experience performance degradation. The authors compare two scenarios: "replace," where models are trained only on synthetic data from the preceding model, leading to collapse, and "accumulate," where all real and synthetic data are retained, avoiding collapse. They extend this analysis to three new generative modeling settings (multivariate Gaussian, kernel density estimation, and supervised finetuning of language models), finding the same contrast. A compromise scenario, "accumulate-subsample," where data accumulates but models are trained under a fixed compute budget, shows intermediate performance, with losses plateauing rather than diverging. Finally, the study reveals a non-trivial interaction between real and synthetic data: synthetic data is beneficial when real data is scarce but harmful when real data is plentiful.

## Key Claims

*   Model collapse occurs when models are trained on evolving datasets built by deleting past data en masse ("replace" scenario).
*   Model collapse is avoided when training datasets accumulate all real and synthetic data ("accumulate" scenario).
*   This contrast between "replace" and "accumulate" holds across multivariate Gaussian modeling, kernel density estimation, and supervised finetuning of language models.
*   In an "accumulate-subsample" scenario (accumulating data with a fixed compute budget per model), model performance on real test data is better than in the "replace" scenario but worse than in the "accumulate" scenario, with losses plateauing instead of diverging.
*   The value of synthetic data for reducing test loss depends on the quantity of real data: synthetic data improves performance when real data is scarce but harms performance when real data is plentiful.

## Contribution to Research Frame

This source directly supports the inferred research frame by providing empirical and theoretical evidence for the conditions under which model collapse occurs and can be mitigated. It confirms that model collapse is a "manageable risk dependent on data hygiene" and the "ratio of real-to-synthetic data." The "accumulate" scenario, which preserves real data, is shown to avoid collapse, aligning with the idea of maintaining distributional stability. The findings on the interaction between real and synthetic data also highlight the importance of the "ratio of real-to-synthetic data" for preventing collapse. The paper's exploration of the "accumulate-subsample" scenario, where performance plateaus rather than diverges, suggests that even with computational constraints, collapse is not inevitable if data accumulates. The study's findings on the beneficial effects of synthetic data when real data is scarce also offer a nuanced perspective on its role, suggesting it can be a tool for improving models under specific conditions, rather than solely a cause of collapse.
