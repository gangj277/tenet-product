# AI Model Collapse: Causes and Prevention

**Publication:** WitnessAI Blog
**Date:** November 24, 2025

## Summary

This article from WitnessAI explains the emerging risk of "AI model collapse," a phenomenon where large language models (LLMs) degrade over time due to recursive training on AI-generated data. This process leads to a loss of data diversity, accuracy, and meaning, resulting in incoherent, biased, or nonsensical outputs. The article details the causes, consequences, and signs of model collapse, emphasizing that it is a preventable risk through better data governance, model oversight, and intelligent training strategies, such as prioritizing human-generated data and tracking data provenance.

## Key Claims

*   AI model collapse occurs when models are trained on data generated by other AI systems, leading to a progressive degradation of performance.
*   This recursive training process results in a loss of data diversity, accuracy, and meaning, causing outputs to become incoherent, biased, or repetitive.
*   Consequences include a loss of output quality, amplification of biases, erosion of real-world alignment, degradation of future AI generations, and potential economic and security impacts.
*   Causes include recursive data training, reduced access to original human-generated data, synthetic data contamination, feedback loops in data aggregation, and a lack of provenance tracking.
*   Signs of model collapse include decreased output diversity, higher factual inaccuracy, unnatural phrasing, loss of novelty, and reduced correlation to training objectives.
*   Model collapse is not inevitable and can be prevented through strategies like prioritizing human-generated data, tracking data history (provenance), continuous data accumulation, using high-quality validated synthetic data, and implementing AI governance tools.

## Relevant Evidence/Quotes

*   "AI model collapse refers to the progressive degradation of an AI model’s performance caused by training on data that has been generated by other AI systems rather than original, human-generated content."
*   "When a new model is trained primarily or partially on AI-generated data — such as text or images produced by previous LLMs — it inherits the biases, errors, and distortions embedded in those outputs."
*   "Over multiple training cycles, recursively generated data causes statistical distortions known as data distribution drift."
*   "The concept of model collapse was formally explored in the 2023 paper ‘The Curse of Recursion: Training on Generated Data Makes Models Forget’ by Ilia Shumailov, Zakhar Shumaylov, and Yiren Zhao."
*   "Preventing model collapse requires a multi-layered data management and governance strategy that prioritizes diversity, transparency, and ongoing validation."
