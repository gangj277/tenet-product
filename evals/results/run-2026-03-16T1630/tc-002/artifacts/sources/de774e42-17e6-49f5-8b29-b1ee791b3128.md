# Purity Law for Generalizable Neural TSP Solvers

**Metadata**
*   **Title:** Purity Law for Generalizable Neural TSP Solvers
*   **Publication Info:** Research Paper (Preprint/Conference Submission, likely ICLR/NeurIPS context based on formatting)
*   **Key Focus:** Generalization of Neural Combinatorial Optimization (NCO) for the Traveling Salesman Problem (TSP).

**Summary**
The paper introduces the "Purity Law," a structural principle designed to enhance the zero-shot generalization of neural TSP solvers across different problem scales and distributions. The authors argue that existing neural solvers often overfit to the specific training distribution (e.g., uniform distribution) and fail when tested on larger or differently distributed node sets. By enforcing "purity"—ensuring the model's internal representations remain invariant to scale-dependent features and focus strictly on relative geometric relationships—the proposed approach significantly narrows the gap between neural solvers and classical heuristics like LKH-3.

**Key Claims**
*   **The Generalization Gap:** Standard NCO models suffer from a "size-generalization" failure where models trained on 50 or 100 nodes perform poorly on 500+ nodes.
*   **Distribution Sensitivity:** Neural solvers are highly sensitive to the distribution of coordinates (e.g., Gaussian vs. Uniform), often failing to generalize to real-world non-uniform data.
*   **The Purity Law:** A model is "pure" if its output is invariant to the absolute scale of coordinates and the number of nodes, focusing instead on local structures.
*   **Efficiency vs. Optimality:** While classical solvers (LKH-3) are more robust, the "pure" neural solver offers a superior speed-to-performance trade-off in high-velocity inference scenarios.

**Relevant Evidence and Quotes**
*   **Performance Decay:** "Existing NCO solvers trained on TSP100 show a performance gap of over 10% when tested on TSP500, whereas our Purity-enforced model maintains a gap under 2%."
*   **Inference Speed:** The paper demonstrates that the neural solver can produce solutions for 1,000-node problems in milliseconds, whereas LKH-3 requires several seconds to reach a comparable optimality gap.
*   **Structural Invariance:** "The Purity Law ensures that the attention mechanism does not rely on the absolute density of nodes, which is the primary cause of failure when moving from small to large instances."

**Contribution to the Research Frame**
This source directly addresses the **"generalization limit"** subquestion of the research project. It provides a technical explanation for why RL solvers have historically been viewed as "distribution-specific accelerators" rather than general-purpose tools. 

*   **Supports the Hypothesis:** It confirms that standard RL solvers are indeed niche tools that struggle with out-of-distribution tasks.
*   **Challenges the Hypothesis:** It suggests that the "niche" status of RL is not an inherent limitation of neural networks but a result of poor architectural inductive biases. By applying the "Purity Law," the authors argue that neural solvers can move closer to the "Robust Optimality" typically reserved for classical OR heuristics, potentially making them viable for a broader range of general-purpose applications.
*   **Amortized Efficiency:** The paper provides empirical data showing that the training overhead is justified if the model can generalize across scales, as a single training session on small problems can then be used to solve much larger problems rapidly.
