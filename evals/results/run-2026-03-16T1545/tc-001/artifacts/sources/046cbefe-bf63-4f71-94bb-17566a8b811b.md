# Research Source Summary: AI models collapse when trained on recursively generated data

**Title:** AI models collapse when trained on recursively generated data  
**Authors:** Shumailov, I., Shumaylov, Z., et al.  
**Publication:** *Nature* (2024/2025)  
**Data/Code Availability:** [GitHub/Zenodo](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.10866595)

---

### Summary
This foundational study explores "model collapse," a degenerative process where generative models trained on data produced by previous AI generations lose the ability to represent the true underlying data distribution. The authors demonstrate through mathematical proofs and empirical experiments (GMMs, VAEs, and LLMs) that recursive training causes the "tails" of a distribution to disappear, eventually leading the model to converge toward a low-variance point estimate (a delta function) that misperceives reality. The research concludes that without a steady influx of original human-generated data or rigorous data provenance, the value of future web-scraped datasets will diminish, potentially stalling the advancement of large-scale AI.

### Key Claims
*   **Inevitability of Collapse:** Model collapse is a universal phenomenon across generative architectures (LLMs, VAEs, GMMs) when they are trained indiscriminately on their own outputs.
*   **Three Sources of Error:** Collapse is driven by the compounding of (1) **Statistical approximation error** (finite sample size), (2) **Functional expressivity error** (model capacity limits), and (3) **Functional approximation error** (limitations in learning procedures/algorithms).
*   **Stages of Degradation:** The process typically moves from "early model collapse" (loss of information about rare/tail events) to "late model collapse" (the model converges to a distribution with little resemblance to the original, often showing near-zero variance).
*   **First-Mover Advantage:** Organizations that trained models on the "clean" pre-AI internet have a significant advantage over those attempting to train new models on an internet increasingly "polluted" by synthetic content.
*   **Mitigation via Data Mixing:** Preserving even a small percentage (e.g., 10%) of original human data can significantly delay, though not necessarily permanently prevent, the onset of collapse.

### Relevant Evidence & Quotes
*   **Mathematical Proof:** "The Markov chain contains at least one absorbing state... the only absorbing states are those corresponding to delta functions. As a result... we are guaranteed to end up in a constant state, having lost all the information of the original distribution."
*   **LLM Empirical Result:** In fine-tuning OPT-125m on its own outputs without original data, perplexity increased from 20 to 28 over generations, with outputs becoming repetitive and nonsensical (e.g., a list of jackrabbit colors replacing architectural descriptions).
*   **The "Tail" Problem:** "Preserving the ability of LLMs to model low-probability events is essential to the fairness of their predictions: such events are often relevant to marginalized groups."
*   **Data Value:** "Data about human interactions with LLMs will be increasingly valuable... access to the original data distribution is crucial."

### Contribution to Research Frame
This source provides the **primary technical validation** for the "inevitability" side of the research project's hypothesis. It identifies the mathematical drivers of collapse, specifically highlighting that even under "ideal" conditions (no functional error), statistical sampling noise alone is sufficient to trigger degradation. 

*   **Supports the Frame:** By providing a mathematical proof that models converge to delta functions, it establishes a "fundamental limit" to recursive training.
*   **Challenges/Refines the Frame:** The ablation study showing that 10% data preservation mitigates the worst effects suggests that "collapse" is a **manageable engineering challenge** rather than an immediate catastrophe, provided data hygiene and provenance (tagging AI vs. human data) are strictly maintained. It shifts the research focus from "will it collapse?" to "what is the minimum ratio of human data required to prevent it?"
